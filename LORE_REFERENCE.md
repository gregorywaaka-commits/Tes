## Calendar & Time

- Tamriel uses **Eras** as the major unit. A new Era begins whenever a significant shift in power reshapes the world (founding of an Empire, its collapse, etc.).
- Within an era, dates are written as `<Era abbreviation> <year>`, e.g. `2E 572`, `3E 427`.
- **Era abbreviations:** ME = Merethic Era, 1E = First Era, 2E = Second Era, 3E = Third Era, 4E = Fourth Era.
- The in-game calendar has **12 months** of 30 days + 3 intercalary days. Year length ≈ 365 days.

## Historical Timeline

### Dawn Era
- Exists outside linear time; events are mythic and non-linear.
- The et'Ada (original spirits) shape creation. Some sacrifice their immortality to create Mundus (Aedra); others refuse (Daedra).
- **Convention at the Adamantine Tower** (Ada-Mantia): the Aedra meet and establish the rules of Mundus. This marks the end of the Dawn Era.

### Merethic Era (ME)
- **ME ~2500:** Construction of Ada-Mantia (Adamantine/Direnni Tower), oldest structure in Tamriel.
- Aldmer (High Elves) arrive from Aldmeris (Old Ehlnofey), spread across Tamriel and diverge into Altmer, Chimer, Bosmer, Falmer, Dwemer, etc.
- Humans (Nedes/Atmorans) begin migrating from Atmora to Tamriel's north.
- Crystal Tower (Summerset) and White-Gold Tower (Cyrodiil) constructed in the Middle/Late Merethic Era.
- **ME ~1000:** Ysgramor leads the Atmorans. **Night of Tears** — Falmer (Snow Elves) destroy Saarthal; Ysgramor returns with the **500 Companions** to retake it.
- **ME 1:** Camoran Dynasty founded in Valenwood → marks end of Merethic Era.

## Races of Tamriel

### Mer (Elves)

**Altmer (High Elves)**
- Homeland: Summerset Isles (Alinor)
- Features: Tallest elves; golden skin; extremely long-lived
- Culture: Hierarchical, elitist, closest descent from the Aedra. Masters of magic. Aldmeri Dominion is their political arm.
- Religion: Worship Auri-El (= Akatosh), Magnus, Trinimac (now Malacath), Syrabane, others

**Bosmer (Wood Elves)**
- Homeland: Valenwood
- Features: Short; agile; keen senses; superior archers
- Culture: Follow the **Green Pact** — cannot harm vegetation; must eat fallen enemies within 3 days (ritual cannibalism). Highly animistic.
- Religion: Y'ffre is their primary deity (god of the forest)

**Dunmer (Dark Elves)**
- Homeland: Morrowind
- Features: Ash-grey skin; red eyes; fire-resistant
- Culture: Once Chimer (golden-skinned followers of Boethiah, Mephala, Azura). Cursed grey by Azura after the Tribunal betrayed Nerevar. Organized into **Great Houses**. Deep ancestor worship.
- Religion: Tribunal (Vivec, Almalexia, Sotha Sil) + The Good Daedra (Azura, Boethiah, Mephala) + ancestor spirits

**Orsimer (Orcs)**
- Homeland: Orsinium (Wrothgarian Mountains, High Rock/Hammerfell border)
- Features: Green/grey skin; tusked; physically powerful
- Culture: **Code of Mauloch** — strict honor system. Clan-based. Skilled smiths (Orcish armor). Repeatedly persecuted and rebuilt Orsinium.
- Religion: Malacath (formerly Trinimac, corrupted by Boethiah)
- Note: Orsinium was destroyed 1E 416, 2E 430s, and again later. Current iteration in Wrothgar region.

**Falmer (Snow Elves)**
- Formerly snow-white elves of Skyrim; driven underground by Ysgramor. Enslaved by Dwemer and degenerated into feral creatures over millennia.
- Original Snow Elves (e.g. Knight-Paladin Gelebor in Dawnguard) remain sentient.

**Dwemer (Deep Elves)**
- Homeland: Dwemer ruins across Morrowind, Hammerfell, Skyrim
- Features: Technologically advanced; bronze-skinned; master of tonal architecture and automatons (Animunculi)
- Culture: Skeptical rationalists; rejected the divinity of the et'Ada. Used tonal magic and steam-powered constructs.
- **Disappeared entirely at the Battle of Red Mountain (c. 1E 700)** — exact cause unknown. Theories involve the Heart of Lorkhan and Kagrenac's Tools.

### Men (Humans)

**Nord**
- Homeland: Skyrim (originally from Atmora)
- Features: Tall, fair; cold-resistant; strong
- Culture: Warrior culture; honor and strength. Dragon tongue tradition; Greybeards. Clan-based society.

**Imperial (Cyrodiilic)**
- Homeland: Cyrodiil
- Features: Diplomatic; adaptable
- Culture: Created the Empire of Tamriel. Skilled administrators, traders, soldiers. Absorb other cultures readily.

**Redguard**
- Homeland: Hammerfell (originally from Yokuda)
- Features: Dark-skinned; athletic; poison/disease resistant
- Culture: Warrior culture centered on individual honor. Two factions: Crowns (traditional) and Forebears (pro-Imperial). Master swordsmen; the **Ansei** (sword-singers) could manifest weapons from sheer will.

**Breton**
- Homeland: High Rock
- Features: Mixed human-elven ancestry (Manmeri); magical aptitude
- Culture: Feudal kingdoms; intrigue; magic. Bretons have natural magic resistance from their elven heritage. Reachfolk (Briarheart cults) are a distinct sub-culture.

### Beastfolk

**Argonian**
- Homeland: Black Marsh (Argonia)
- Features: Reptilian; amphibious; disease-resistant; can breathe underwater
- Culture: Deeply tied to the **Hist** (sentient trees that share collective consciousness). Hist can modify Argonian biology. An-Xileel faction fought off the Daedra during the Oblivion Crisis.

**Khajiit**
- Homeland: Elsweyr
- Features: Feline; appearance varies with lunar birth cycle (17+ morphological forms, from small Suthay to massive Senche-Raht)
- Culture: Nomadic in the north; urban in the south. **Moons (Masser and Secunda)** determine birth form. Follow Two Moons Path; the **Mane** is their spiritual leader (one living Mane at a time).

### Extinct / Rare Races
- **Tsaesci** — Akaviri serpent-people; conquered much of Akavir
- **Ka Po'Tun** — tiger-folk of Akavir; oppose Tsaesci; their leader Tosh Raka aspires to become a dragon
- **Lilmothiit** — fox-folk of Black Marsh; believed extinct by 2E
- **Aylieds (Wild Elves)** — ruled Cyrodiil until Alessian Rebellion; largely exterminated or assimilated
- **Keptu** — proto-human inhabitants of High Rock; distinct from Redguards

## The Nine Divines (Aedra)

The Aedra are et'Ada who sacrificed part of their divine substance to help create Mundus. They are more distant than Daedra and cannot directly enter the mortal plane easily.

The **Eight Divines** pantheon was created by **Saint Alessia** to unify the religions of liberated humans. Talos was added later as the Ninth.

| Divine | Also Known As | Domain | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Akatosh** | Auri-El (Altmer), Alkosh (Khajiit), Alduin (corrupted Nord form) | Time, endurance, legitimacy | Chief of the pantheon; Dragon God of Time; symbol of the Empire |
| **Arkay** | Arkay the Enemy (Undead perspective) | Life, death, burial rites, seasons | Sometimes said to have been a mortal once |
| **Dibella** | — | Beauty, love, art, music | Goddess of beauty and sometimes sexual instruction |
| **Julianos** | — | Wisdom, logic, law, literature, magic | God of scholars and wizards |
| **Kynareth** | Kyne (Nords) | Nature, sky, winds, rain | Nord form = Kyne, "mother of men"; patron of sailors |
| **Mara** | — | Love, compassion, fertility, marriage | Universal mother goddess; in some cultures a warrior-goddess |
| **Stendarr** | — | Mercy, justice, righteous might | God of righteous rule; patron of the Vigil of Stendarr |
| **Zenithar** | Z'en (Bosmer) | Work, commerce, wealth | God of honest labor; patron of merchants |
| **Talos** | Tiber Septim, Ysmir, Hjalti Early-Beard | War, state, governance, human achievement | Deified mortal (Tiber Septim); ⚠️ Worship banned by White-Gold Concordat (4E 22); Aldmeri Dominion denies his divinity |

**Note on Talos:** Three Nords merged into Tiber Septim according to some accounts (Hjalti Early-Beard, General Zurin Arctus, Ysmir Wulfharth). This is highly metaphysical/contested.

## Vampire Bloodlines

**Origin:** Molag Bal **assaulted** Lamae Beolfag (a Nedic monk) in the Merethic Era, creating the first vampire: **Lamae Bal** (also called the Blood Matron). Her curse spread and mutated into regional bloodlines. All Tamrielic vampires descend from this origin.

**Disease names:** Porphyric Hemophilia (Morrowind), Sanguinare Vampiris (Cyrodiil/Skyrim).

| Bloodline | Region | Focus | Creation | Notable Details |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Lamae Bal** | Tamriel-wide (origin) | Progenitor | Merethic Era | The original bloodline; "purest" form; all others descend from this |
| **Volkihar** | Skyrim | Martial / cold | 1E or earlier | Lurk under frozen lakes; Nordic origin; pure-bloods can become Vampire Lords |
| **Vampyrum Order** | **Cyrodiil** | Noble / intrigue | 1E | ⚠️ CYRODILIC, not Morrowind. Court-predators; embedded in Imperial society; servants of Molag Bal |
| **Quarra Clan** | Morrowind (NW Vvardenfell, Druscashti) | Warrior | Pre-3E | Most physically aggressive Morrowind clan; membership leans Nordic |
| **Aundae Clan** | Morrowind (W Vvardenfell, Ashmelech) | Mage | Pre-3E | Elitist spellcasters; use illusion and mysticism; membership leans Altmeri |
| **Berne Clan** | Morrowind (S Vvardenfell, Galom Daeus) | Stealth / assassin | Pre-3E | Masters of ambush and poison; membership leans Imperial |
| **Gray Host** | Hammerfell / Bangkorai (2E) | Vampire-werewolf army | Late 1E (c. 1E 1029) | Led by King Styriche; allied with Molag Bal; **defeated at Bangkorai Garrison** |

**Key lore points:**
- The three Morrowind clans (Quarra, Aundae, Berne) are **hostile to each other**.
- Vampires are reviled in Dunmeri society; living openly is impossible.
- The Morrowind clans are in decline by 3E 427.
- **Vampire Lords** are apex bloodline members with near-unkillable power; a separate trait from base bloodlines.

## Werewolves & Lycanthropy

- Created by **Hircine**, the Daedric Prince of the Hunt.
- Lycanthropy is passed through bites.
- Werewolves go to Hircine's **Hunting Grounds** when they die (not Sovngarde or other afterlives).
- The **Companions** of Whiterun have a secret tradition of werewolfism within their inner Circle.
- **Gray Host** (c. 1E 1029) combined vampires and werewolves in a single army.
- **Hircine** views lycanthropes as his property and his finest hunt.

## Great Houses of Morrowind

The Dunmer are organized into **Great Houses** — noble-political clans that govern Morrowind. Originally six existed; House Dagoth was destroyed. The **Grand Council** coordinates between Houses.

| House | Role/Archetype | Territory | Status | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Redoran** | Warriors / Knights | NW Morrowind, Ald'ruhn | Active → dominant post-Red Year | Duty, honor, piety. After Red Year (4E 5), became dominant House and leads Grand Council. |
| **Telvanni** | Mages / Isolationists | NE Morrowind, Tel Vos, Tel Fyr | Active | Personal power, independence. Loosely organized; masters live in mushroom towers. Tolerant of necromancy and Daedra worship. |
| **Hlaalu** | Merchants / Diplomats | Central Morrowind, Balmora, Narsis | Stripped of Great House status (post-4E 5) | Embraced Imperial rule; prospered under it; lost status after Red Year discredited Imperial alignment. |
| **Indoril** | Priests / Temple guardians | Central/Southern Morrowind, Mournhold | Weakened | Closely tied to Tribunal Temple; supplied Ordinators. Severely weakened after Tribunal's fall and Red Year. |
| **Dres** | Agricultural / Slavers | SE Morrowind, Tear | Active | Slave-based economy (Argonian slaves primarily). Extremely conservative. Eventually forced to restructure under external pressure. |
| **Dagoth (Sixth House)** | Heretics / Cultists | Under Red Mountain | **Destroyed** (3E 427) | Destroyed after War of the First Council; Dagoth Ur survived and resurrected the Sixth House underground. Defeated by Nerevarine in TES III. |

**Important dates:**
- **War of the First Council** — Chimer/Dwemer joint council breaks down; Battle of Red Mountain follows (c. 1E 700)
- **Red Year (4E 5)** — Red Mountain eruption reshapes Morrowind politics; Hlaalu falls; Redoran rises
- **House Sadras** — emerged as a new Great House after the Red Year, replacing Hlaalu

## Major Guilds & Factions

### Mages Guild
- Founded: **c. 2E 230** by **Vanus Galerion**
- Purpose: Unify magical practitioners; regulate magic; academic research
- Headquarters: Arcane University, Imperial City (3E)
- Dissolved: after Oblivion Crisis (4E 1+)
- Successor: College of Winterhold (Skyrim), various independent academies

### Fighters Guild
- Imperial institution; monster hunting and mercenary contracts
- Dissolved or became inactive in Skyrim; replaced by **the Companions**
- Active across Tamriel through 2E–3E

### Thieves Guild
- Also: Children of the Shadows
- Tenets: No killing during theft; do not steal from the poor
- Operates from Riften (Skyrim era) via "The Ragged Flagon"
- Patrons: **Nocturnal** (the Nightingales are a secret inner circle sworn to Nocturnal)

### Dark Brotherhood
- Worship: **Sithis** (the void/chaos) and the **Night Mother**
- Purpose: Contract murder
- Hierarchy: Listener → Speaker → Keeper → Silencer → Initiate
- Tenets: Five Tenets (do not steal from a Brother, do not kill a Brother, etc.)
- ⚠️ Whether the Night Mother IS Mephala is **contested canon** — "The Brothers of Darkness" states some believe this; others deny it. Not confirmed as official.

### Companions
- Skyrim's answer to the Fighters Guild
- Founded in tradition of **500 Companions of Ysgramor**
- Led by the **Harbinger** (not a commander — a guide)
- **The Circle** (inner council) are secretly werewolves via Hircine's gift

### Psijic Order
- Ancient magical order from the island of **Artaeum**
- Predate the Mages Guild by centuries
- Artaeum disappeared c. 2E 230 (when Galerion left to found Mages Guild) and **returned 2E 580**
- Guard the Elder Scrolls in TES V: Skyrim's Dawnguard/College questline

### Morag Tong
- Dunmer assassination guild; legal in Morrowind
- Patron: **Mephala**
- Unlike Dark Brotherhood, operates openly (writs of execution are legal documents)
- Rival/predecessor organization to the Dark Brotherhood

### Tribunal Temple
- State religion of Morrowind under the Third Empire
- Worships the **Tribunal** (Vivec, Almalexia, Sotha Sil) as living gods
- **Ordinators** are its enforcers
- Declined after TES III Morrowind events revealed the Tribunal's mortality

## Cosmology & Metaphysics

### The Mundus
- The mortal plane created by the sacrifice of the Aedra.
- Consists of **Nirn** (the world) + the moons **Masser** (Jone) and **Secunda** (Jode) + **Aetherius** (the magical energy realm beyond)
- Held together by the **Towers**.

### Aetherius vs. Oblivion
- **Aetherius**: The realm of pure magic; where the gods went after creation; the source of magic in Nirn. The stars are holes in Oblivion through which Aetherius light shines.
- **Oblivion**: The void outside Mundus; home of the Daedra. Each Daedric Prince rules a pocket realm.

### The et'Ada
- Original spirits who existed before Mundus.
- Those who helped create Mundus and sacrificed divine substance → **Aedra** (our ancestors).
- Those who refused → **Daedra** (not our ancestors).

### Lorkhan (Shor/Sep/Shezarr)
- A trickster et'Ada who convinced the Aedra to create Mundus.
- His "heart" (the Heart of Lorkhan) fell to Nirn and became the **Stone of the Red Tower**.
- Worshipped by Nords as **Shor**, by Imperials as **Shezarr**, hated by Altmer as **Sep** (the serpent who stole the stars).

### The Dragon Break
- A period where linear time shatters; multiple contradictory timelines exist simultaneously.
- Caused by major metaphysical disruptions (Numidium's activation at the end of Daggerfall, etc.).
- The "Warp in the West" (Daggerfall's resolution) is a Dragon Break.

### CHIM
- A state of perfect self-awareness within the dream of creation.
- Achieving CHIM allows a person to exist outside the normal rules of Mundus without ceasing to exist.
- **Tiber Septim** and **Vivec** are believed to have achieved CHIM.
