## Mages Guild `[CANON — UESP]`
> **Source:** UESP Lore:Mages Guild — https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Mages_Guild

- **Founded:** c. **2E 230** by **Vanus Galerion** on Summerset Isle `[CANON — UESP]`
- **Purpose:** Unify magical practitioners across Tamriel; regulate magic use; provide spell merchants; conduct magical research; perform public charity work
- **Leadership:** **Arch-Mage** at the top; **Council of Mages** as senior body; local Guildmasters per city
- **Headquarters (3E):** **Arcane University**, Imperial City, Cyrodiil `[CANON — TES IV: Oblivion]`
- **Prohibition:** Official ban on **necromancy** within Guild premises (though the definition and enforcement varied widely; a major source of internal conflict in TES IV). `[CANON — TES IV: Oblivion]`
- **Dissolution:** After the **Oblivion Crisis (3E 433)**, the Mages Guild collapsed due to the death of the Arch-Mage, public distrust of mages, and Imperial instability. `[CANON — UESP]`
- **Successor:** **College of Winterhold** (Skyrim) fills the void for magical education in Skyrim specifically. `[CANON — TES V: Skyrim]` No direct Empire-wide successor exists in 4E. `[CANON — UESP]`
- **Conflict with Necromancers Guild:** The **Order of the Black Worm** (Mannimarco's necromancers) were a persistent rival and enemy throughout the Guild's history. `[CANON — TES IV: Oblivion]`

## Fighters Guild `[CANON — UESP]`
> **Source:** UESP Lore:Fighters Guild — https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Fighters_Guild

- **Purpose:** Monster hunting, mercenary contracts, enforcement services for hire; a semi-official regulator of contract fighting
- **Leadership:** **Guildmaster** overall; **Chapter Masters** locally; membership ranked by skill
- **Active era:** 2E–3E primarily; spread throughout the Empire
- **Code of conduct:** Members follow a strict code; contracts are regulated; random violence is prohibited
- **Dissolution:** No longer present in Skyrim by 4E 201; presumed dissolved or inactive in many regions after the Oblivion Crisis. `[CANON — UESP]`
- **Successor:** The **Companions** (Whiterun, Skyrim) fill a similar role in that province; not an official continuation. `[CANON — TES V: Skyrim]`

## Companions `[CANON — TES V: Skyrim]`
> **Source:** TES V: Skyrim (Companions questline); UESP Lore:Companions

- **Based:** **Jorrvaskr** mead-hall, **Whiterun**, Skyrim
- **Founded:** Tradition traces back to **Ysgramor's 500 Companions** (Merethic Era), though the current organisation is a much-later descendant rather than a direct continuation `[CANON — UESP]`
- **Purpose:** Contract work, protection, warrior training; the Nordic equivalent of a mercenary guild — but with heavy cultural and honour traditions
- **Leadership:** **Harbinger** — not a commander but a guide; the Companions are theoretically a brotherhood of equals
- **The Circle:** Inner council of senior members; secretly **werewolves** through a pact with Hircine. `[CANON — TES V: Skyrim]` Regular members are unaware of this. `[CANON — TES V: Skyrim]`
- **Active era:** 4E (TES V: Skyrim); pre-dated the Fighters Guild in Skyrim and outlasted it `[CANON — TES V: Skyrim]`

## Thieves Guild `[CANON — UESP]`
> **Source:** UESP Lore:Thieves Guild — https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Thieves_Guild  
> **Also called:** Children of the Shadows (in some eras)

- **Purpose:** Organised crime — theft, smuggling, bribery, fencing stolen goods; provides structure to criminal enterprises and "regulates" theft within cities
- **Leadership:** **Guild Master** overall; local leadership varies by city
- **Tenets (code of conduct):** Do not kill during theft; do not rob the poor; do not steal from other Guild members; do not deal with **Skooma** trade `[CANON — TES V: Skyrim]`
- **Presence in Skyrim (4E):** Operates from **The Ragged Flagon**, a tavern inside the **Ratway** beneath **Riften**. By 4E 201 the Guild is a shadow of its former self. `[CANON — TES V: Skyrim]`
- **The Nightingales:** A secret inner circle of three members who pledge their soul to **Nocturnal** (Daedric Prince of Night/Luck) in exchange for power. They are bound to guard the **Twilight Sepulchre** in death. `[CANON — TES V: Skyrim]`
- **Notes:** The Guild tolerates — or is tolerated by — local authorities in many cities due to pragmatic crime-regulation benefits. `[CANON — UESP]`

## Dark Brotherhood `[CANON — UESP]`
> **Source:** UESP Lore:Dark Brotherhood — https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dark_Brotherhood  
> **Source:** TES IV: Oblivion (Dark Brotherhood questline)  
> **Source:** TES V: Skyrim (Dark Brotherhood questline)  
> **Source:** In-game book: The Brothers of Darkness

- **Purpose:** Contract assassination; murder for hire
- **Religion:** Worship **Sithis** (the void / primordial chaos; the empty set before creation) and follow the **Night Mother** (their divine intermediary). `[CANON — UESP]`
- **Organisation:** Each regional **Sanctuary** is headed by a **Listener** (who hears the Night Mother's voice) and **Speakers** (who receive contracts). Ranks: Listener → Speaker → Keeper → Silencer → Initiate. `[CANON — TES IV: Oblivion; UESP]`
- **The Five Tenets:** `[CANON — TES IV: Oblivion]`
  1. Never kill an innocent outside of a contract
  2. Never betray the Brotherhood
  3. Never steal from a Brother or Sister
  4. Never harm a Brother or Sister except in self-defence
  5. Never disobey or disrespect the Night Mother (or a superior)
- **Status in 4E (Skyrim):** Most Sanctuaries have been destroyed (by the Penitus Oculatus — the Empire's secret police). Only the **Falkreath Sanctuary** remains functional. `[CANON — TES V: Skyrim]`
- **Night Mother / Mephala controversy:** Some texts (including in-game book **"The Brothers of Darkness"**) state that the Night Mother IS **Mephala**. ⚠️ This is `[CONTESTED]` — the book presents it as the belief of some Brotherhood members, not confirmed canon. Other sources treat the Night Mother as a distinct entity (a mortal woman who sacrificed her five children to Sithis). **Do not treat this as settled lore.**

## Morag Tong `[CANON — TES III: Morrowind]`
> **Source:** UESP Lore:Morag Tong — https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Morag_Tong  
> **Source:** TES III: Morrowind (Morag Tong questline)

- **Purpose:** Assassination; BUT **legally sanctioned** in Morrowind — writs of execution are legal documents. This distinguishes them from the Dark Brotherhood. `[CANON — TES III: Morrowind]`
- **Patron:** **Mephala** `[CANON — TES III: Morrowind]`
- **Headquarters:** **Vivec City** (Hall of Justice area), Morrowind
- **History:** Predate the Dark Brotherhood; the Brotherhood may have split off from or been inspired by the Morag Tong, though the exact historical relationship is `[CONTESTED — UESP]`.
- **Notes:** Operating openly as an assassin guild is unique to Morrowind. The Tong perform **ritual killings** (Honored Ancestor killings) for Great Houses to resolve disputes and prevent open warfare. `[CANON — TES III: Morrowind]`
- **Rivalry with Dark Brotherhood:** The Morag Tong considers the Dark Brotherhood heretics; the Brotherhood in turn fears the Tong. `[CANON — TES III: Morrowind; UESP]`

## Psijic Order `[CANON — UESP]`
> **Source:** UESP Lore:Psijic Order — https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Psijic_Order

- **Based:** Island of **Artaeum** (location shifts in and out of normal reality)
- **Purpose:** Ancient magical order; practitioners of the **Old Ways** (pre-Mages Guild magic); guardians of knowledge and the Elder Scrolls
- **Founded:** Predates the Mages Guild by centuries; considered the oldest order of mages in Tamriel `[CANON — UESP]`
- **Key dates:**
  - Artaeum **disappears c. 2E 230** (when Vanus Galerion leaves the Order to found the Mages Guild) `[CANON — UESP]`
  - Artaeum **returns c. 2E 580** `[CANON — UESP]` ⚠️ Previously had a mod error placing this differently; 2E 580 is correct.
- **Abilities:** Psijics can manipulate time (time stop ability in TES V: Skyrim), traverse planes, and access knowledge unavailable to others. `[CANON — TES V: Skyrim; UESP]`
- **Relation to Mages Guild:** Historically separate and often in tension; the Psijics considered the Guild's approach to magic too undisciplined. `[CANON — UESP]`

## Tribunal Temple `[CANON — TES III: Morrowind]`
> **Source:** UESP Lore:Tribunal Temple — https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Tribunal_Temple  
> **Source:** TES III: Morrowind (Temple questline and texts)

- **Based:** Morrowind; temples in all major cities; Great Temple in **Vivec City**
- **Purpose:** State religion of Morrowind; worship the **Tribunal** (Vivec, Almalexia, Sotha Sil) as living gods
- **Enforcers:** **Ordinators** (Temple guards/inquisitors); most come from **House Indoril** `[CANON — TES III: Morrowind]`
- **Decline:** After the Nerevarine defeats Dagoth Ur and the Tribunal's divinity ends (3E 427), the Temple transitions from god-worship to **ancestor worship** of the Tribunal as historical figures. `[CANON — TES III: Morrowind — Tribunal expansion]`
- **Relation to Houses:** Closely intertwined with **House Indoril** (see `08_great_houses.md`). The Temple's political influence shaped all House relations. `[CANON — TES III: Morrowind]`

## Imperial Cult `[CANON — TES III: Morrowind]`
> **Source:** UESP Lore:Imperial Cult — https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Imperial_Cult

- **Based:** Imperial outposts and garrisons across the Empire, including in Morrowind
- **Purpose:** Spread worship of the **Nine Divines** in all Imperial territories; a soft-power arm of Imperial expansion
- **Conflict with Tribunal Temple:** In Morrowind, the Imperial Cult and Tribunal Temple existed in uneasy parallel — the Empire allowed Dunmer to keep their religion as a condition of the **Armistice**. `[CANON — TES III: Morrowind]`

## Vigil of Stendarr `[CANON — TES V: Skyrim / Dawnguard]`
> **Source:** TES V: Skyrim (Dawnguard DLC); UESP Lore:Vigil of Stendarr

- **Based:** **Hall of the Vigilant** (Skyrim); chapters across Tamriel
- **Purpose:** Hunt and destroy **Daedra, vampires, werewolves**, and all supernatural threats to mortals in the name of Stendarr
- **Status in 4E 201 (Skyrim):** The Hall of the Vigilant is **destroyed by Lord Harkon's vampires** at the start of Dawnguard. The Vigil is severely weakened. `[CANON — TES V: Dawnguard]`

## Dawnguard `[CANON — TES V: Dawnguard]`
> **Source:** TES V: Skyrim — Dawnguard DLC

- **Based:** **Fort Dawnguard**, Rift, Skyrim (a remote mountain fort)
- **Purpose:** Hunt and destroy vampires specifically; ancient order revived by **Isran** in response to the Volkihar vampire threat
- **Founder (original):** Ancient order; disbanded at some point. Revived by Isran in 4E 201. `[CANON — TES V: Dawnguard]`
