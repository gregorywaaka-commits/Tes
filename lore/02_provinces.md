## Overview `[CANON — UESP]`

Tamriel has **nine provinces**, each dominated by a primary race and distinct geography. At various points all nine have been unified under the Empire; political control shifts frequently across eras. Islands and sub-regions exist but are not counted as full provinces.

---

## The Nine Provinces

### Skyrim `[CANON — UESP]`
> **Source:** UESP Lore:Skyrim — https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Skyrim

- **Location:** Far north
- **Primary inhabitants:** Nords
- **Climate/geography:** Arctic and subarctic; rugged mountains, ancient pine forests, tundra plains. Highest peak: **Throat of the World** (Snow Throat Tower site).
- **Major cities:** Solitude (traditional High King seat), Windhelm, Whiterun, Markarth, Riften
- **Notes:** Called the "Old Kingdom" or "Fatherland" of the Nords. Origin of the **Way of the Voice (Greybeards)**, founded c. 1E 1069. Dragon worship was practiced by ancient Nords before being suppressed.

### Cyrodiil `[CANON — UESP]`
> **Source:** UESP Lore:Cyrodiil — https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Cyrodiil

- **Location:** Central Tamriel
- **Primary inhabitants:** Imperials
- **Climate/geography:** Temperate forest and fertile plains (Nibenay Valley); the Great Forest in the west; Lake Rumare surrounds the Imperial City island.
- **Major cities:** Imperial City (seat of the Ruby Throne), Anvil, Bravil, Bruma, Chorrol, Kvatch, Leyawiin, Skingrad
- **Notes:** Heart of the Empire. White-Gold Tower is located here on the Imperial City isle. Originally ruled by Ayleids before the Alessian Rebellion (1E 243). During the **Interregnum (2E 430–896)** Cyrodiil was a battleground of warring warlords; notably, the **Longhouse Emperors** (2E 533–577) — a Reachman dynasty founded by **Durcorach the Black Drake** — briefly controlled the Ruby Throne before being overthrown by Duke **Varen Aquilarios** in the Colovian Revolt (Varen ruled only briefly before the Soulburst and disappearance). `[CANON — UESP: Lore:Longhouse Emperors]`

### Morrowind `[CANON — UESP]`
> **Source:** UESP Lore:Morrowind — https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Morrowind

- **Location:** Northeast Tamriel
- **Primary inhabitants:** Dunmer (Dark Elves)
- **Climate/geography:** Ashlands, volcanic terrain; the island of **Vvardenfell** contains **Red Mountain**; Inner Sea separates Vvardenfell from the mainland.
- **Major cities:** Vivec City (3E, largely destroyed 4E 5), Mournhold/Almalexia (mainland capital), Blacklight (post-Red Year capital)
- **Notes:** Governed by the **Great Houses** system. Red Mountain erupts in 4E 5, dramatically reshaping the province. Home to the **Dunmer**, once the **Chimer** (before Azura's curse at the Battle of Red Mountain).

### Hammerfell `[CANON — UESP]`
> **Source:** UESP Lore:Hammerfell — https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Hammerfell

- **Location:** West Tamriel
- **Primary inhabitants:** Redguards
- **Climate/geography:** Dominated by the **Alik'r Desert**; coastal cities with access to both the Iliac Bay and the Eltheric Ocean.
- **Major cities:** Sentinel (northern capital), Stros M'Kai (island port), Hegathe (southern capital)
- **Notes:** Redguards arrived from **Yokuda** (sank c. 1E 792). Two political factions: **Crowns** (traditional; prefer old Yokudan ways) and **Forebears** (more open to Imperial influence). `[CANON — UESP]` Hammerfell **refused to sign the White-Gold Concordat** (4E 22) and fought the Aldmeri Dominion to a stalemate separately. `[CANON — TES V: Skyrim in-game books]`

### High Rock `[CANON — UESP]`
> **Source:** UESP Lore:High Rock — https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:High_Rock

- **Location:** Northwest Tamriel
- **Primary inhabitants:** Bretons (and significant Orsimer populations)
- **Climate/geography:** Temperate; rolling hills, dense forests, long coastlines on the Iliac Bay. Isle of **Balfiera** (home of the Adamantine Tower) is here.
- **Major cities:** Daggerfall, Wayrest, Camlorn, Evermore
- **Notes:** Bretons are **Manmeri** — human-elven mixed ancestry from ancient Aldmeri influence. Many rival feudal kingdoms; constant political intrigue. The **Reachfolk** (Briarheart cultists, hagraven pact-makers) are a distinct indigenous sub-culture in the eastern Reach. `[CANON — ESO, UESP]`
- **Keptu** — proto-human inhabitants of High Rock; NOT ancestors of Redguards. `[CANON — UESP]` ⚠️ Previously confused with Redguard ancestors in some fan sources; this is wrong.

### Summerset Isles (Alinor) `[CANON — UESP]`
> **Source:** UESP Lore:Summerset Isles — https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Summerset_Isles

- **Location:** Far southwest, island chain
- **Primary inhabitants:** Altmer (High Elves)
- **Climate/geography:** Lush subtropical; rolling green hills, fertile farmland, pristine coastlines. Crystal-Like-Law (Crystal Tower) stood here until its destruction (ESO era).
- **Major cities:** Alinor (capital; name also used for the whole province by the Altmer), Shimmerene, Lillandril
- **Notes:** Called **Summerset** by Imperial cartographers; the Altmer call it **Alinor**. Extremely xenophobic historically. Home of the **Altmeri Dominion / Thalmor**. Aldmer originally settled here first before dispersing across Tamriel. `[CANON — UESP]`

### Valenwood `[CANON — UESP]`
> **Source:** UESP Lore:Valenwood — https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Valenwood

- **Location:** Southwest Tamriel
- **Primary inhabitants:** Bosmer (Wood Elves)
- **Climate/geography:** Densely forested, massive rainforests; little infrastructure; coastlines with mangrove swamps. The graht-oak trees can uproot and walk.
- **Major cities:** **Falinesti** (the walking city — a giant graht-oak that migrates with the seasons), Silvenar, Elden Root
- **Notes:** Bosmer follow the **Green Pact** — cannot harm vegetation; must eat fallen enemies within 3 days. Falinesti is the Green-Sap Tower site. `[CANON — UESP]`

### Elsweyr `[CANON — UESP]`
> **Source:** UESP Lore:Elsweyr — https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Elsweyr

- **Location:** South-central Tamriel
- **Primary inhabitants:** Khajiit
- **Climate/geography:** Two distinct halves — **Anequina** (arid desert badlands, north) and **Pellitine** (lush tropical jungle/rivers, south). Khajiit culture and appearance differ between halves.
- **Major cities:** Rimmen (Anequina), Riverhold, Torval (Pellitine capital), Senchal (port)
- **Notes:** Elsweyr is the **union of two former kingdoms** — **Anequina** (north; arid, tribal) and **Pellitine** (south; lush, mercantile) — merged in **2E 309** when Keirgo of Anequina married Eshita of Pellitine. Political stability was achieved through the **Riddle'Thar** governance system (2E 311), overseen by the **Mane**, which balanced power according to the phases of the two moons. `[CANON — UESP: Lore:Elsweyr Confederacy]` ⚠️ The merger was a political marriage/treaty, NOT an Imperial decree. The Khajiit's physical form is determined by the phases of Tamriel's two moons (**Masser** and **Secunda**) at birth; 17+ morphological forms exist. `[CANON — IN-GAME BOOK: Words of Clan Mother Ahnissi]`

### Black Marsh (Argonia) `[CANON — UESP]`
> **Source:** UESP Lore:Black Marsh — https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Black_Marsh

- **Location:** Southeast Tamriel
- **Primary inhabitants:** Argonians
- **Climate/geography:** Vast swamps, wetlands, rainforests; nearly impassable by non-Argonians due to disease and terrain. Interior called the **Hist-Grove** regions.
- **Major cities:** Gideon (Imperial-built border city), Lilmoth, Thorn
- **Notes:** Argonians are deeply bonded to the **Hist** (sentient trees with shared consciousness). The **An-Xileel** faction repelled Oblivion's armies during the Crisis by taking the fight into Oblivion. `[CANON — TES IV: Oblivion lore / UESP]` The interior of Black Marsh has never been fully mapped by outsiders. `[CANON — UESP]`
- **Knahaten Flu (2E 560–c.603):** A devastating plague originating in Stormhold wiped out virtually all non-Argonian inhabitants of Black Marsh, including the **Kothringi** and **Lilmothiit** peoples. Argonians were largely immune. The disease spread to neighbouring provinces, killing millions. `[CANON — UESP: Lore:Knahaten Flu; in-game book: On the Knahaten Flu]` ⚠️ Whether the flu was natural, engineered by Argonian shamans, or Daedric in origin is `[CONTESTED]` — no single explanation is confirmed canon.

## Adjacent Regions (Not Provinces) `[CANON — UESP unless noted]`

| Region | Notes |
|---|---|
| **Solstheim** | Island north of Morrowind; disputed between Skyrim and Morrowind administratively. Events of TES III: Bloodmoon and TES V: Dragonborn. |
| **Thras** | Island SW of Summerset; homeland of the **Sload** (slug-like necromancers). Site of the **Coral Tower**. `[CANON — UESP]` The Sload unleashed the **Thrassian Plague (1E 2200)**, killing more than half of Tamriel's population. In retaliation the **All Flags Navy (1E 2241–2260)** — the largest multi-national fleet in Tamrielic history, led by Baron-Admiral Bendu Olo of Anvil — besieged Thras and sank the island chain (1E 2260) when the Coral Tower fell and a massive whirlpool consumed the fleet and the Sload homeland. Ships dragged under are said to dwell in Coldharbour as the "Lost Fleet." `[CANON — UESP: Lore:All Flags Navy; Lore:Thrassian Plague]` ⚠️ Thras re-emergence in this mod is a `[MOD CHOICE]` — canonical Thras was sunk c. 1E 2260, though it eventually surfaces again in later ages. |
| **Yokuda** | Former Redguard homeland; mostly sunk c. 1E 792. Site of the **Orichalc Tower** (destroyed with Yokuda). `[CANON — UESP]` |
| **Akavir** | Eastern continent; home of four known races. Source of multiple Tamrielic invasions. `[CANON — IN-GAME BOOK: Mysterious Akavir; UESP: Lore:Akavir]` All knowledge of Akavir reaches Tamriel through second-hand sources — Tamrielic records are unreliable guides to the full truth.<br>**Tsaesci** ("Snake Palace") — vampiric serpent-folk; tall, covered in golden scales, effectively immortal. The dominant power of Akavir. Said to have "eaten" all the men of Akavir (whether this is literal consumption, conquest, or cultural absorption is `[CONTESTED]`). Their martial culture introduced **katana-style weaponry** to Tamriel. They invaded Tamriel in 1E 2703, were defeated by Reman I at Pale Pass, and subsequently swore loyalty to him as the Dragonguard (forerunners of the Blades). A Tsaesci ruling class (**Akaviri Potentate**) then governed the Empire from 1E 2920–2E 430. In 2E 572, a Kamal commander **Dir-Kamal** led a separate Akaviri invasion of Morrowind and Skyrim — he was killed at the Battle of Vivec's Antlers by the Ebonheart Pact. `[CANON — UESP: Lore:Tsaesci; Lore:Akaviri Potentate]`<br>**Ka Po'Tun** ("Tiger-Dragon Isles") — tiger-folk cat-people whose god-king **Tosh Raka** is claimed to have transformed himself into a dragon (orange-and-black). They fight constantly with the Tsaesci and have allied with the Tang Mo. Were formerly known as the Po'Tun before their cultural evolution. `[CANON — UESP: Lore:Ka Po'Tun; IN-GAME BOOK: Mysterious Akavir]`<br>**Tang Mo** ("Thousand Monkey Isles") — many breeds of monkey-folk on scattered islands; described as kind, brave, and sometimes "terrifyingly mad" in battle. Frequently targeted by Kamal invasions but repeatedly drive them back. Allied with the Ka Po'Tun. `[CANON — UESP: Lore:Tang Mo; IN-GAME BOOK: Mysterious Akavir]`<br>**Kamal** ("Snow Hell") — armies of snow demons who freeze solid in winter and thaw in summer, at which point they invade neighbours. Always defeated. The most famous commander, **Ada'Soom Dir-Kamal** ("King of Kamal"), led the 2E 572 invasion of Tamriel. `[CANON — UESP: Lore:Kamal; IN-GAME BOOK: Mysterious Akavir]`<br>⚠️ Akavir has never been successfully conquered by Tamriel. Two Tamrielic expeditions (Uriel V's invasion 3E 271–288) reached Akavir but ultimately failed. `[CANON — UESP: Lore:Akavir]` |
| **Atmora** | Northern continent; original homeland of the Nords/Atmorans. Now frozen and uninhabitable. `[CANON — UESP]` |
| **Aldmeris** (Old Ehlnofey) | Legendary original homeland of the Elves; location unknown or may no longer exist. `[CONTESTED — UESP]` Some accounts say it sank; others say it never existed as a physical place. |
