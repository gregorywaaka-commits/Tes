## Ysgramor `[CANON — UESP]`
> **Source:** UESP Lore:Ysgramor — https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ysgramor

- **Era:** Merethic Era
- **Role:** Atmoran hero; first King of Skyrim; founder of the Companions tradition
- **Key facts:**
  - Led the original Atmoran settlement of Saarthal; survived the **Night of Tears** when the Falmer/Snow Elves destroyed it `[CANON — TES V: Skyrim]`
  - Returned from Atmora with the **500 Companions** to retake Skyrim `[CANON — TES V: Skyrim]`
  - First person to write in a human language (the Nordic translation of Elvish) `[CANON — IN-GAME BOOK: Songs of the Return]`
  - His tomb is at **Ysgramor's Tomb**, Skyrim `[CANON — TES V: Skyrim]`
- **Cultural significance:** Nords revere Ysgramor as their founding father; he is Sovngarde-bound and a near-mythic figure.

## Saint Alessia `[CANON — UESP]`
> **Source:** UESP Lore:Alessia — https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Alessia  
> **Source:** In-game book: The Adabal-a

- **Era:** 1E 243+
- **Role:** First Empress; leader of the Alessian Slave Rebellion; saint of the Imperial cult
- **Key facts:**
  - Born a **Nedic slave** under Ayleid masters in Cyrodiil `[CANON — UESP]`
  - Prayed to **Akatosh**; the Dragon God appeared and gave her the **Amulet of Kings** and the **Dragonfires covenant** `[CANON — IN-GAME BOOK: The Adabal-a]`
  - Led the rebellion that overthrew Ayleid rule; took White-Gold Tower 1E 243 `[CANON — UESP]`
  - Created the **Eight Divines** pantheon to unify her people's religious traditions `[CANON — UESP]`
  - Died 1E 266; the Amulet of Kings passed to her heirs `[CANON — UESP]`
  - Revered as a saint in the Imperial Cult `[CANON — TES IV: Oblivion]`

## Reman Cyrodiil `[CANON — UESP]`
> **Source:** UESP Lore:Reman Cyrodiil — https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Reman_Cyrodiil

- **Era:** c. 1E 2200s
- **Role:** Founder of the Second Empire (Reman Dynasty)
- **Key facts:**
  - Rose to prominence c. 1E 2200, uniting the fractured kingdoms of Cyrodiil `[CANON — UESP]`
  - Defeated the **Akaviri invasion** of 1E 2703 and incorporated the surviving Akaviri into his armies (they became the **Akaviri Potentate** — elite Imperial guard) `[CANON — UESP]`
  - United all of Tamriel under his rule **except Morrowind and Summerset** `[CANON — UESP]`
  - His dynasty lasted until 1E 2920 when **Reman III** was assassinated by Morag Tong agents
- **Notes:** Often considered the greatest pre-Septim emperor. His empire was more successful militarily than Alessia's. `[SOFT CANON — historian POV, UESP]`

## Nerevar `[CANON — UESP, but details CONTESTED]`
> **Source:** UESP Lore:Nerevar — https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nerevar  
> **Source:** In-game book: Nerevar at Red Mountain  
> **Source:** In-game book: The Battle of Red Mountain (Vivec's version)

- **Era:** c. 1E 700
- **Role:** Chimer general; Hortator (supreme war-chief) of the Chimer; leader of the First Council
- **Key facts:**
  - United the **Chimer** and **Dwemer** in the First Council against Nordic invaders `[CANON — UESP]`
  - Led the **Chimer-Dwemer coalition** at the **Battle of Red Mountain (c. 1E 700)** `[CANON — UESP]`
  - His death is the single most contested event in TES lore:
    - **Version 1 (Tribunal account):** Died heroically in the battle; the Tribunal used Kagrenac's Tools only after his death and with his blessing `[IN-GAME BOOK: Vivec's version]`
    - **Version 2 (Ashlander oral tradition):** Was **murdered by the Tribunal** after the battle so they could use the Heart without opposition `[IN-GAME BOOK: Nerevar at Red Mountain]`
    - **Version 3 (Dagoth Ur's account):** Dagoth Ur himself was loyal; it was the others who betrayed Nerevar `[TES III: Morrowind, Dagoth Ur dialogue]`
  - ⚠️ All three versions are presented as potentially true within TES lore. **The truth is deliberately left ambiguous by the developers.** `[CONTESTED — by design]`
- **Reincarnation:** The **Nerevarine** (player character of TES III: Morrowind) is the prophesied reincarnation of Nerevar. `[CANON — TES III: Morrowind]`

## The Tribunal: Vivec, Almalexia, Sotha Sil `[CANON — UESP]`
> **Source:** UESP Lore:Tribunal — https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Tribunal  
> **Source:** TES III: Morrowind + Tribunal expansion

### Vivec
- Poet-warrior god of Morrowind; one of the most complex figures in TES lore
- Wrote the **36 Lessons of Vivec** (in-game metaphysical/philosophical texts — deliberately unreliable narrators `[CONTESTED by design]`)
- Held back **Baar Dau** (a moon the Sheogorath flung at Vivec City) with his divine will; when his power failed after 3E 427, Baar Dau eventually fell (4E 5 Red Year) `[CANON — UESP]`
- Disappeared after the events of TES III: Morrowind; fate unknown `[CANON — TES III: Morrowind]`
- ⚠️ Whether Vivec achieved **CHIM** is `[CONTESTED — SPECULATION]`

### Almalexia
- Goddess of compassion and motherhood; patron of **House Indoril** and **Ordinators**
- After losing her divinity in TES III: Morrowind (Tribunal expansion), went mad and was killed by the Nerevarine in Mournhold `[CANON — TES III: Tribunal]`

### Sotha Sil
- "The Clockwork God"; Tinkerer; philosopher; rarely seen by mortals even when divine
- Created the **Clockwork City** — a mechanical world hidden in Oblivion `[CANON — TES III: Tribunal; ESO: Clockwork City DLC]`
- Killed by **Almalexia** before the events of TES III: Tribunal expansion `[CANON — TES III: Tribunal]`

## Dagoth Ur `[CANON — TES III: Morrowind]`
> **Source:** TES III: Morrowind (main quest); UESP Lore:Dagoth Ur

- **Era:** 1E 700 → 2E 882 (awakening) → 3E 427 (defeat)
- **Role:** Lord of **House Dagoth** (the Sixth House); primary antagonist of TES III: Morrowind
- **Key facts:**
  - Originally **Lord Voryn Dagoth**, loyal companion of Nerevar at Red Mountain `[CANON — TES III: Morrowind]`
  - After the battle, merged with the **Heart of Lorkhan** and gained divine power of his own `[CANON — TES III: Morrowind]`
  - Slept beneath Red Mountain for over a millennium; **awakened in 2E 882** `[CANON — TES III: Morrowind; UESP]`
  - Spread **Corprus disease** and **blight storms** from Red Mountain `[CANON — TES III: Morrowind]`
  - Also spread **Sleeper agents** — mortals mentally dominated through dream-connection to the Heart `[CANON — TES III: Morrowind]`
  - Defeated by the **Nerevarine** in 3E 427 when the Heart of Lorkhan was destroyed `[CANON — TES III: Morrowind]`

## Tiber Septim (Talos) `[CANON — UESP, some details CONTESTED]`
> **Source:** UESP Lore:Tiber Septim — https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Tiber_Septim  
> **Source:** In-game book: The Arcturian Heresy

- **Era:** 2E 896–3E 38
- **Role:** First Emperor of the Third Era; deified posthumously as **Talos** (Ninth Divine)
- **Key facts:**
  - United Tamriel c. 2E 896 using the **Numidium** (Walk-Brass Tower golem) `[CANON — UESP]`
  - Known by multiple names: Tiber Septim, **Ysmir** (Nordic name), Hjalti Early-Beard (possible birth name `[CONTESTED — The Arcturian Heresy]`)
  - Founded the Third Empire; his lineage (the **Septim Dynasty**) ruled until 3E 433 `[CANON — UESP]`
  - Achieved divine status after death; became **Talos**, the Ninth Divine `[CANON — Imperial theology; CONTESTED by Aldmeri Dominion]`
  - ⚠️ "Three Septims in one" theory (Hjalti + Zurin Arctus + Ysmir Wulfharth merged into Tiber Septim): `[SOFT CANON — The Arcturian Heresy; contested by mainstream accounts]`

## Jurgen Windcaller `[CANON — UESP]`
> **Source:** UESP Lore:Jurgen Windcaller — https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Jurgen_Windcaller  
> **Source:** In-game book: Songs of the Return

- **Era:** c. 1E 1069
- **Role:** Nordic thu'um master; founder of the **Way of the Voice** (Greybeards)
- **Key facts:**
  - Founded the Way of the Voice approximately **369 years after the Battle of Red Mountain (c. 1E 700)** = c. **1E 1069** `[CANON — UESP]`
  - After defeating armies in battle using the Voice, reconsidered the use of the Thu'um as a weapon; concluded the Voice should be used only to honour the gods, not for warfare `[CANON — IN-GAME BOOK: Songs of the Return; TES V: Skyrim]`
  - ⚠️ A previous mod error placed Jurgen Windcaller at **1E 416** — this is **wrong**. 1E 1069 is correct.
  - Meditated for 40 years inside Throat of the World after founding the Way `[CANON — TES V: Skyrim]`

## Martin Septim `[CANON — TES IV: Oblivion]`
> **Source:** TES IV: Oblivion (main quest); UESP Lore:Martin Septim

- **Era:** 3E 433
- **Role:** Last Emperor of the Septim line; Avatar of Akatosh
- **Key facts:**
  - Illegitimate son of **Emperor Uriel Septim VII**; was a priest of Akatosh, unaware of his heritage `[CANON — TES IV: Oblivion]`
  - Used the **Amulet of Kings** and **sacrificed himself** to summon **Akatosh's avatar**, which destroyed Mehrunes Dagon and ended the Oblivion Crisis `[CANON — TES IV: Oblivion]`
  - His sacrifice destroyed the Amulet and ended the Dragonfires covenant; no further Septim line exists `[CANON — TES IV: Oblivion]`
  - Turned to a **stone dragon statue** at the Temple of the One (which still stands in the Imperial City) `[CANON — TES IV: Oblivion]`

## Dir-Kamal `[CANON — ESO / UESP]`
> **Source:** ESO in-game book: The Second Akaviri Invasion; UESP

- **Era:** 2E 572
- **Role:** Kamal (Akaviri / snow demon) invasion leader
- **Key facts:**
  - Led a **Kamal army** that attacked Morrowind and Skyrim in 2E 572 `[CANON — UESP]`
  - **KILLED** (not merely driven back) at the **Battle of Vivec's Antlers** by the combined Ebonheart Pact forces and the Tribunal `[CANON — ESO in-game book: The Second Akaviri Invasion]`
  - ⚠️ A previous mod error described Dir-Kamal as "driven back" — he was **killed**. This matters for event flavour text accuracy.

## Mannimarco `[CANON — UESP]`
> **Source:** UESP Lore:Mannimarco — https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Mannimarco  
> **Source:** TES II: Daggerfall; TES IV: Oblivion; ESO

- **Era:** 2E (ESO) → 3E (Daggerfall, Oblivion)
- **Role:** "King of Worms"; Lich; leader of the **Order of the Black Worm** (necromancers)
- **Key facts:**
  - Was once an **Altmer** student at the **Psijic Order** before being expelled for necromancy `[CANON — UESP]`
  - Founded the **Order of the Black Worm** as an anti-Mages Guild necromancer organisation `[CANON — TES IV: Oblivion]`
  - Became a **Lich** (undead immortal spellcaster) and later an **Ascended Immortal** `[CANON — TES II: Daggerfall]`
  - ⚠️ The ESO version of Mannimarco (2E) and the Daggerfall/Oblivion versions (3E) represent the same individual across different eras but are different depictions. The ESO version is mortal-ish; the later version is a full Lich/ascended. `[CANON]`

## Arngeir `[CANON — TES V: Skyrim, but used as 4E-specific flavour in this mod]`
> **Source:** TES V: Skyrim; UESP Lore:Arngeir

- **Era:** 4E 201
- **Role:** Master Greybeard at **High Hrothgar**, Skyrim; one of four surviving masters of the Thu'um
- **Key facts:**
  - A 4E figure; exists in TES V: Skyrim's present-day setting `[CANON — TES V: Skyrim]`
  - Is not a historical figure available during the 2E or 3E periods covered by most of this mod's events
  - ⚠️ Using Arngeir as flavour text for earlier eras is a **`[MOD CHOICE]`** — an intentional use of a known name for recognition purposes, not a claim that he was alive then.
