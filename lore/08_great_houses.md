## Overview `[CANON — UESP]`

The **Great Houses** of Morrowind are the noble-political clan structures that govern Dunmeri society. They function simultaneously as noble houses, political parties, religious factions, and military orders. Each controls specific territories and maintains its own armies, courts, and bureaucracies. `[CANON — UESP]`

The **Grand Council** coordinates between Houses on matters affecting all of Morrowind. `[CANON — TES III: Morrowind]`

Originally **six Houses** existed (Redoran, Telvanni, Hlaalu, Indoril, Dres, and Dagoth). All trace their origins to ancient **Velothi clans** — the followers of the prophet **Veloth** who led the Chimer out of Summerset to settle Morrowind in the Merethic Era. `[CANON — UESP]`

The **First Council** (combining Chimer and Dwemer Houses) governed Morrowind jointly until the **War of the First Council** and the **Battle of Red Mountain (c. 1E 700)**. `[CANON — UESP]`

## The Six Original Great Houses

### House Redoran `[CANON — UESP]`
> **Source:** UESP Lore:House Redoran — https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:House_Redoran  
> **Source:** TES III: Morrowind in-game texts

- **Archetype:** Warriors / Knights
- **Core values:** Duty, honour, piety, martial excellence, self-sufficiency
- **Territory:** Northwest Morrowind; Vvardenfell's northwest region (Balmora area is contested with Hlaalu; Ald'ruhn is their Vvardenfell capital)
- **Structure:** Strict hierarchy led by the most capable and honourable warriors; councillors selected by merit within the honour framework
- **Politics:** Conservative; resistant to Imperial influence; put Morrowind and the Tribunal Temple first. Often in conflict with pragmatic Hlaalu. `[CANON — TES III: Morrowind]`
- **Post-Red Year (4E 5):** Redoran **assumed leadership of the Grand Council** after the Red Year devastated Morrowind. Now the dominant House. `[CANON — TES V: Dragonborn context, UESP]`
- **Patron saint:** St. Veloth `[CANON — UESP]`
- **Motto:** "Our honour is our life." `[CANON — IN-GAME BOOK: Mottos of the Dunmeri Great Houses]`

### House Telvanni `[CANON — UESP]`
> **Source:** UESP Lore:House Telvanni — https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:House_Telvanni  
> **Source:** TES III: Morrowind in-game texts

- **Archetype:** Mages / Isolationists / Anarchists
- **Core values:** Personal magical power, independence, self-reliance, ambition — the strong rule, the weak serve
- **Territory:** Northeast Morrowind; distinctive **mushroom tower** settlements (Tel Vos, Tel Fyr, Tel Aruhn, etc.)
- **Structure:** Extremely loose — each master-wizard (councillor) acts independently and largely ignores central authority. Promotions are earned by assassination or demonstrating superior magical power (in practice). `[CANON — TES III: Morrowind]`
- **Politics:** Profoundly isolationist; distrustful of outsiders and non-Dunmer. Rarely cooperate with other Houses unless personally advantageous. `[CANON — TES III: Morrowind]`
- **Attitudes to magic:** Tolerant of necromancy and Daedra worship where other Houses are not. One councillor (**Divayth Fyr**) experiments with Corprus disease. `[CANON — TES III: Morrowind]`
- **Post-Red Year:** Weathered the Red Year well due to their isolationism and powerful individual members. Remain active. `[CANON — UESP]`
- **Motto:** "Our secrets are our power." `[CANON — IN-GAME BOOK: Mottos of the Dunmeri Great Houses]`

### House Hlaalu `[CANON — UESP]`
> **Source:** UESP Lore:House Hlaalu — https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:House_Hlaalu  
> **Source:** TES III: Morrowind in-game texts

- **Archetype:** Merchants / Diplomats / Political opportunists
- **Core values:** Wealth, adaptability, commerce, diplomacy — prosperity through flexible ethics
- **Territory:** Central Morrowind; Balmora (Vvardenfell capital), Narsis (mainland)
- **Structure:** Meritocratic in theory — those who bring wealth and influence are promoted. In practice the **Camonna Tong** (Hlaalu's criminal arm) and Imperial connections drive advancement. `[CANON — TES III: Morrowind]`
- **Politics:** Most pro-Imperial of all Houses; embraced the Third Empire's arrival and profited enormously from Imperial trade relations. `[CANON — TES III: Morrowind]`
- **Post-Red Year:** Imperial alignment discredited them after the Empire failed to protect Morrowind during the Red Year. **Stripped of Great House status** by the Grand Council and replaced by **House Sadras**. `[CANON — UESP]`
- **Motto:** "We should prove profitable to House Hlaalu and profitable to our clients." `[CANON — IN-GAME BOOK: Mottos of the Dunmeri Great Houses]`

### House Indoril `[CANON — UESP]`
> **Source:** UESP Lore:House Indoril — https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:House_Indoril  
> **Source:** TES III: Morrowind in-game texts

- **Archetype:** Priests / Tribunal Temple guardians / Administrative nobility
- **Core values:** Faith, duty to the Tribunal, tradition, Dunmeri heritage
- **Territory:** Central and southern Morrowind; Mournhold/Almalexia (the mainland Tribunal city); many Tribunal Temple properties
- **Structure:** Closely intertwined with the **Tribunal Temple** — most Ordinators (Temple guards/inquisitors) come from Indoril. Effectively the civil service and priesthood combined. `[CANON — TES III: Morrowind]`
- **Politics:** Conservative and theocratic; strongly resistant to Imperial religious influence. The merger of House and Temple gave Indoril enormous influence as long as the Tribunal remained divine. `[CANON — TES III: Morrowind]`
- **Post-Tribunal collapse + Red Year:** Severely weakened. The fall of the Tribunal removed their religious mandate; the Red Year destroyed their territory. Many Indoril nobles became priests or retreated into obscurity. `[CANON — UESP]`
- **Motto:** "All who oppose the Tribunal and the law of Morrowind are our enemies." `[CANON — IN-GAME BOOK: Mottos of the Dunmeri Great Houses]`

### House Dres `[CANON — UESP]`
> **Source:** UESP Lore:House Dres — https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:House_Dres  
> **Source:** TES III: Morrowind in-game texts

- **Archetype:** Agricultural lords / Slavers
- **Core values:** Agriculture, tradition, wealth through land and **slavery**, contempt for outsiders
- **Territory:** Southeast Morrowind; the **Deshaan Plains** (rich farmland); capital at **Tear** on the coast near Black Marsh
- **Structure:** Plantation oligarchy; slave labour (primarily **Argonian**) underpins their economy. `[CANON — TES III: Morrowind]`
- **Politics:** Extremely conservative; opposed to Imperial anti-slavery laws. Primary conductors of the **slave trade** in Morrowind. Located close to Black Marsh specifically to raid Argonians. `[CANON — UESP]`
- **Post-Red Year:** Survived the Red Year better than coastal Houses. Eventually forced to restructure slave operations under sustained external pressure (Empire, An-Xileel Argonian resistance). `[CANON — UESP]`
- **Motto:** "We rule through the wisdom of tradition and the strength of our land." `[CANON — IN-GAME BOOK: Mottos of the Dunmeri Great Houses]`

### House Dagoth (The Sixth House) `[CANON — UESP]`
> **Source:** UESP Lore:House Dagoth — https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:House_Dagoth  
> **Source:** TES III: Morrowind (entire main quest)

- **Archetype:** (Formerly) legitimate Great House → Heretic cult
- **Core values:** (As currently constituted) devotion to **Dagoth Ur**; blight and Corprus as spiritual gifts; driving out the Tribunal and Empire from Morrowind
- **Territory:** The underground complex beneath **Red Mountain** (the Heart chamber and surrounding caverns called **Kogoruhn** on the surface)
- **Original House:** Once a legitimate and powerful House that guarded the Heart of Lorkhan alongside Nerevar. **Betrayed the Tribunal** at the Battle of Red Mountain (c. 1E 700) — or the Tribunal betrayed them, depending on source. `[CONTESTED — UESP]`
- **Dagoth Ur's survival:** **Lord Voryn Dagoth** (Dagoth Ur) survived by merging with the Heart of Lorkhan, gaining divine power of his own. He slept under Red Mountain for over a millennium, **awakening in 2E 882**. `[CANON — TES III: Morrowind; UESP]`
- **Blight and Corprus:** Dagoth Ur spreads **Corprus disease** and **blight storms** from Red Mountain. Corprus makes the afflicted physically powerful but destroys the mind — except in the Nerevarine, whose immunity is what makes them the chosen one. `[CANON — TES III: Morrowind]`
- **Destruction:** Defeated by the **Nerevarine** in 3E 427 when the Heart of Lorkhan's connection to the mortal plane was severed — the Nerevarine used Kagrenac's Tools to break the enchantments binding the Heart to Mundus, causing it to vanish from Tamriel. This severing stripped Dagoth Ur of his divine power and killed him. The Heart itself was not destroyed (a divine artifact cannot be annihilated); it departed the mortal world. `[CANON — TES III: Morrowind; UESP Lore:Heart of Lorkhan]`
- **Current status:** **Destroyed.** No longer a functioning House. `[CANON — TES III: Morrowind]`
- **Mod note:** In this mod, early triggers for Dagoth Ur awakening fire at 2E 600 (if no Red Mountain Tower keeper) and 2E 700 (if no Dunmer kingdom); canonical full awakening = 2E 882. `[MOD CHOICE aligned with lore]`

## Post-Red Year Changes `[CANON — UESP]`

| Change | Details |
|---|---|
| **House Hlaalu** stripped of Great House status | Replaced by **House Sadras** (a newer house) |
| **House Redoran** now leads the Grand Council | Dominant military and political force |
| **House Indoril** severely weakened | Many nobles became monks or retreated |
| **House Sadras** rises | New Great House replacing Hlaalu; details sparse `[SOFT CANON]` |
| Morrowind capital moves | From Vivec City (largely destroyed) to **Blacklight** in the northwest |
