## What Are the Aedra? `[CANON — UESP]`

The **Aedra** ("our ancestors" in Elvish) are et'Ada — original spirits — who chose to participate in the creation of Mundus. This act required sacrificing a portion of their divine nature (their **anuic** power), making them weaker than the Daedra but fundamentally linked to the mortal world. `[CANON — IN-GAME BOOK: On Oblivion]`

Unlike the **Daedra**, Aedra generally cannot directly and physically manifest in Mundus; they work through avatars, champions, and subtle influence. They are largely passive in mortal affairs compared to the more interventionist Daedric Princes. `[CANON — UESP]` ⚠️ This distinction is `[CONTESTED]` in some philosophical texts — some in-universe scholars argue the Aedra are simply Daedra who "lost" and cope by claiming superiority. `[CONTESTED — IN-GAME BOOK: The Monomyth]`

## The Eight Divines (Alessian Pantheon) `[CANON — UESP]`

> **Source:** UESP Lore:Eight Divines — https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Eight_Divines  
> **Note:** Created by **Saint Alessia** c. 1E 243 to unify the Nord and Ayleid/Aldmeri religious traditions of her freed subjects. Each Divine is a syncretic fusion of multiple pre-existing deities. `[CANON — UESP]`

### 1. Akatosh `[CANON — UESP]`
- **Domain:** Time, endurance, invincibility, legitimacy of rulership
- **Also known as:** Auri-El (Altmer), Alkosh (Khajiit), Alduin (corrupted/fragment — Nordic `[CONTESTED]`), Tosh Raka (Ka Po'Tun aspirant title)
- **Symbol:** Dragon
- **Role:** Chief of the pantheon; Dragon God of Time; symbolic foundation of the Empire's right to rule. The **Amulet of Kings** is his covenant with humanity. `[CANON — TES IV: Oblivion]`
- **Notes:** ⚠️ Whether Alduin IS Akatosh or merely a fragment/aspect of him is `[CONTESTED — TES V: Skyrim]`. The Greybeards and Paarthurnax imply they are distinct entities.

### 2. Arkay `[CANON — UESP]`
- **Domain:** Life cycles, birth, death, burial rites, the seasons
- **Also known as:** Xarxes (Altmer, though Xarxes may be a distinct subordinate deity `[CONTESTED]`)
- **Symbol:** A wheel
- **Role:** God of the cycle of life and death; governs proper burial. Undead who circumvent natural death are considered affronts to Arkay.
- **Notes:** Some in-game texts suggest Arkay was once a mortal who achieved divinity, making him an earlier precedent for Talos. `[SOFT CANON — IN-GAME BOOK: The Monomyth]`

### 3. Dibella `[CANON — UESP]`
> **Source:** UESP Lore:Dibella — https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dibella

- **Domain:** Beauty, love, art, music, affection, sexual instruction
- **Also known as:** Mother Dibella, Lady of Love
- **Symbol:** A rose or heart
- **Role:** Goddess of beauty and the arts. Temples to Dibella appear throughout Tamriel. Her cult in Skyrim practices **the Sybil** tradition — a girl is chosen as the living vessel of Dibella's will. `[CANON — TES V: Skyrim]`
- **Sibyls:** In Markarth specifically, Dibella's priestesses are called **Sibyls** (oracles/seers) rather than ordinary clergy. The Sybil is believed to speak with Dibella's voice. `[CANON — TES V: Skyrim, The Forsworn Conspiracy quest]`
- **Dibella's Order:** Maintains temples in Markarth and other major cities. The Order is dedicated to aesthetic transcendence — approaching the divine through beauty and love. `[CANON — UESP]`
- **Worshippers:** Bards, artists, lovers, and those who seek the beautiful. Dibella is patron of all who pursue the transcendent dimension of art and love — she is **NOT** merely a goddess of physical beauty. She represents the transformative power of art and love as pathways to the divine. `[SOURCE: UESP Lore:Dibella]`

### 4. Julianos `[CANON — UESP]`
- **Domain:** Wisdom, logic, literature, law, history, contradiction
- **Also known as:** Jhunal (Nordic, though rarely worshipped in that form)
- **Symbol:** A triangle or crystal
- **Role:** God of scholars, wizards, and logicians. Associated with the Mages Guild and academic pursuit.

### 5. Kynareth `[CANON — UESP]`
- **Domain:** Nature, wind, sky, rain, travellers, sailors
- **Also known as:** Kyne (Nords — considered the "mother of men" in Nordic tradition), Tava (Redguards — bird goddess)
- **Symbol:** A feather or wind symbol
- **Role:** Goddess of the heavens and all natural phenomena. Nordic form **Kyne** is considered the widow of Shor (Lorkhan) and mother figure of humanity. `[CANON — IN-GAME BOOK: The Annotated Anuad]`
- **Notes:** Kyne is considered by Nords to be the true chief deity, not Akatosh. This is a cultural difference, not a contradiction. `[CANON — UESP]`

### 6. Mara `[CANON — UESP]`
- **Domain:** Love, compassion, fertility, marriage, family
- **Also known as:** Mother Mara, the Goddess of Love
- **Symbol:** A flame or maternal figure
- **Role:** Universal mother goddess. Almost universally worshipped across all cultures in varying forms. In some older texts Mara appears as a warrior goddess as well. `[SOFT CANON — IN-GAME BOOK: The Monomyth]`

### 7. Stendarr `[CANON — UESP]`
- **Domain:** Mercy, justice, righteous might, compassion in battle
- **Also known as:** God of Mercy, the Steadfast
- **Symbol:** A hammer or gauntlet
- **Role:** God of righteous rule; patron of the **Vigil of Stendarr** (an order of monster hunters active in the 4E, particularly against vampires and werewolves). `[CANON — TES V: Dawnguard]`

### 8. Zenithar `[CANON — UESP]`
- **Domain:** Work, commerce, wealth through honest labour, industry
- **Also known as:** Z'en (Bosmer — though Z'en may be a distinct deity `[CONTESTED — UESP]`)
- **Symbol:** Scales or a forge
- **Role:** God of merchants and craftspeople. Encourages honest trade and hard work as a form of worship. Most worshipped in trading cities and port towns.

## The Ninth Divine: Talos `[CANON — but CONTESTED in-universe]`

> **Source:** UESP Lore:Talos — https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Talos  
> **Source:** In-game book: The Arcturian Heresy  
> **Source:** In-game book: Talos Worship (various)

- **Domain:** War, governance, state, human achievement, conquest, heroism, unity
- **Also known as:** **Tiber Septim** (mortal name), **Ysmir** ("Dragon of the North" — Nordic name), **Hjalti Early-Beard** (possible birth name `[CONTESTED]`), **Stormcrown**
- **Symbol:** The Stormcrown; a dragon
- **Role:** Deified mortal (Tiber Septim) who achieved godhood through his deeds uniting Tamriel and using the Numidium. Patron of adventurers, generals, and the Empire itself.

### Canon status of Talos's divinity: `[CONTESTED — major in-universe political issue]`
| Position | Source | Status |
|---|---|---|
| Talos IS a Divine; the Ninth | Imperial tradition, Septim dynasty, most humans | `[CANON — established Imperial theology]` |
| Talos is NOT a Divine; was merely a man | Aldmeri Dominion / Thalmor position | `[IN-UNIVERSE CONTESTED — politically motivated]` |
| Talos achieved CHIM and thus transcended mortality | Some philosophical texts | `[SPECULATION — UESP]` |
| Talos is a merger of three individuals | The Arcturian Heresy (in-game book) | `[SOFT CANON — single in-game book source]` |

**Worship status:** After the **White-Gold Concordat (4E 22)**, Talos worship was officially banned across the Empire as part of the peace terms with the Aldmeri Dominion. This is a major source of tension in TES V: Skyrim. `[CANON — TES V: Skyrim]`
