> **Primary source:** UESP Lore:Daedric Princes — https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Daedric_Princes  
> **Secondary:** Elder Scrolls Fandom — https://elderscrolls.fandom.com/wiki/Daedric_Princes

## What Are the Daedric Princes? `[CANON — UESP]`

- **Daedra** ("not our ancestors" in Elvish) are et'Ada who refused to sacrifice their power to help create Mundus. They remain fully divine but exist in **Oblivion** rather than within Mundus.
- The **Daedric Princes** are the most powerful of the Daedra — each rules a **Plane of Oblivion** (their own pocket dimension) and has a defined **Sphere** (the aspect of existence they embody and govern).
- A Prince's Sphere is not merely a "job" — it is the literal essence of their being. They cannot act substantially against their Sphere. `[CANON — UESP]`
- All are referred to as "Princes" regardless of apparent gender or form. `[CANON — UESP]`
- There are **seventeen recognised Princes**. `[CANON — UESP]` ⚠️ Some sources hint at more or fewer; seventeen is the established consensus number.
- Daedric morality is non-human: they are neither good nor evil by mortal standards. Some are relatively benevolent toward mortals (Azura); others are actively hostile (Molag Bal); most are simply indifferent or self-serving. `[CANON — UESP]`

## The Seventeen Daedric Princes

### 1. Azura `[CANON — UESP]`
> **Source:** UESP Lore:Azura — https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Azura

- **Sphere:** Dusk and Dawn, twilight, transitions, prophecy, fate, magic
- **Plane:** Moonshadow (a beautiful realm of flowers and silver skies)
- **Key artifacts:** Azura's Star (grand soul gem that can be reused), Ring of Azura
- **Relation to mortals:** Unusually warm toward her worshippers; considered one of the more "benevolent" Princes — but can be wrathful and unforgiving when disrespected.
- **Relation to Dunmer:** One of the three **Good Daedra** worshipped by the Dunmer. She **cursed the Chimer** into the dark-skinned Dunmer after the Tribunal's betrayal of Nerevar. `[CANON — TES III: Morrowind]`
- **Mod note:** Azura invasion weight = **2** in this mod's Daedric invasion pool. `[MOD CHOICE]`

### 2. Boethiah `[CANON — UESP]`
> **Source:** UESP Lore:Boethiah — https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Boethiah

- **Sphere:** Deceit, conspiracy, assassination, plots of overthrow, treason, unlawful seizure of power
- **Plane:** Attribution's Share (also called Oblivion's Edge in some texts)
- **Key artifacts:** Ebony Mail, Goldbrand, Fearstruck
- **Relation to mortals:** Encourages competition, betrayal, and the overthrow of authority. Values strength and cunning over loyalty.
- **Relation to Dunmer:** One of the three Good Daedra. **Boethiah ate/consumed Trinimac** (the Altmer warrior-god), transforming him into Malacath and his followers into the Orsimer. `[CANON — IN-GAME BOOK: The True Nature of Orcs]` ⚠️ Whether this is literal or metaphorical is `[CONTESTED — UESP]`

### 3. Clavicus Vile `[CANON — UESP]`
> **Source:** UESP Lore:Clavicus Vile — https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Clavicus_Vile

- **Sphere:** Wishes, bargains, power through pacts, trickery through deals
- **Plane:** Clavicus Vile's Realm (a rustic, pleasant-seeming place)
- **Key artifacts:** Masque of Clavicus Vile, Rueful Axe
- **Relation to mortals:** Grants wishes — but always with an ironic, often cruel twist. Delights in the consequences of careless wording.
- **Companion:** Barbas — a shapeshifting dog who is actually a portion of Clavicus Vile's own power. `[CANON — TES V: Skyrim]`

### 4. Hermaeus Mora `[CANON — UESP]`
> **Source:** UESP Lore:Hermaeus Mora — https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Hermaeus_Mora

- **Sphere:** Knowledge, memory, fate, forbidden and unknown lore, destiny
- **Plane:** **Apocrypha** — an infinite realm of black water and floating pages. Everything is dim; towers of stacked tomes rise from the oily water; tentacled creatures called **Lurkers** patrol the depths; **Seekers** (cloaked, face-stealing creatures) protect forbidden tomes. The sky is an eternal grey-green twilight. Every book ever written that contains forbidden knowledge exists here in some form. To enter without Hermaeus Mora's blessing is to risk permanent madness or transformation. `[CANON — TES V: Dragonborn DLC; ESO: Apocrypha chapter; UESP: Lore:Apocrypha]`
- **Key artifacts:** Oghma Infinium (a tome of forbidden knowledge), Black Books (TES V: Dragonborn)
- **Appearance:** Typically manifests as a mass of eyes, tentacles, and mouths — described as fundamentally incomprehensible.
- **Personality:** Patient, curious, obsessive; values the acquisition of knowledge above all else.
- **Mod note:** Hermaeus Mora's character template should use trait **`arbitrary`** — NOT `paranoid`. He is curious and obsessive, not fearful. `[MOD CHOICE confirmed against lore]` ⚠️ Using `paranoid` was a previous mod error, now corrected.

### 5. Hircine `[CANON — UESP]`
> **Source:** UESP Lore:Hircine — https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Hircine

- **Sphere:** The Hunt, the Great Game, the Sport of Princes, lycanthropy
- **Plane:** The Hunting Grounds (an endless primal wilderness)
- **Key artifacts:** Savior's Hide, Ring of Hircine, Spear of Bitter Mercy
- **Relation to mortals:** Created **werebeasts** (werewolves, werebears, werecrocodiles, etc.) as his finest quarry and hunters. Lycanthropes are his "children" and go to the Hunting Grounds when they die rather than their racial afterlife. `[CANON — TES V: Skyrim]`
- **Values:** The chase above the kill. Hircine is honourable by his own code — he respects prey that fights back.

### 6. Jyggalag `[CANON — UESP]`
> **Source:** UESP Lore:Jyggalag — https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Jyggalag

- **Sphere:** Order, logic, absolute predictability, total systematisation
- **Plane:** Unknown (was stripped from him; partially restored after TES IV: Shivering Isles)
- **Key artifacts:** Sword of Jyggalag
- **History:** Jyggalag grew so powerful that the other Daedric Princes feared him and **cursed him to become Sheogorath** — his polar opposite. He could only re-emerge briefly as himself during the **Greymarch** (a periodic event of absolute Order). `[CANON — TES IV: Shivering Isles]`
- **Resolution:** The player defeats Jyggalag in TES IV: Shivering Isles, breaking the curse; Jyggalag departs to reclaim his realm, leaving the player as the new Sheogorath. `[CANON — TES IV: Shivering Isles]`
- **Mod note:** Jyggalag invasion weight = **0 before 3E 400** (base 2, modifier -2). He is barely present before the events of Shivering Isles free him. `[MOD CHOICE aligned with lore]`

### 7. Malacath `[CANON — UESP]`
> **Source:** UESP Lore:Malacath — https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Malacath

- **Sphere:** The ostracised, oaths, curses, the spurned, the abandoned
- **Plane:** Ashpit (a realm of ash, dust, and choking air)
- **Key artifacts:** Volendrung, Scourge
- **Relation to Orcs:** Primary patron deity of the **Orsimer**; his code (*Code of Mauloch*) governs Orc stronghold life. `[CANON — UESP]`
- **Origin:** Once the Aldmeri warrior-god **Trinimac**, transformed/corrupted by Boethiah. `[CANON — IN-GAME BOOK: The True Nature of Orcs]` ⚠️ Whether Malacath IS Trinimac transformed, or is a new entity and Trinimac still exists separately, is `[CONTESTED — UESP]`. Malacath himself denies being Trinimac.

### 8. Mehrunes Dagon `[CANON — UESP]`
> **Source:** UESP Lore:Mehrunes Dagon — https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Mehrunes_Dagon

- **Sphere:** Destruction, change, revolution, ambition, natural disaster
- **Plane:** The Deadlands (a fiery, volcanic realm; home of the **Dremora** and **Xivilai**)
- **Key artifacts:** Mehrunes' Razor (a dagger that instakills on proc), Mysterium Xarxes (a book of dark knowledge)
- **Relation to mortals:** Bringer of change through destruction. Patron of those who seek to tear down existing order and replace it. Primary antagonist of TES IV: Oblivion.
- **Previous invasions:** Multiple attempts to conquer Tamriel throughout history, including the **Oblivion Crisis (3E 433)**. `[CANON]`

### 9. Mephala `[CANON — UESP]`
> **Source:** UESP Lore:Mephala — https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Mephala

- **Sphere:** Lies, plots, secret murder, obfuscation, sex and secret knowledge
- **Plane:** Spiral Skein (a web-like realm)
- **Key artifacts:** Ebony Blade, Ring of Khajiit (shared with Meridia in some sources)
- **Relation to Dunmer:** One of the three Good Daedra; patron of the **Morag Tong** (the legal Dunmer assassination guild). `[CANON — TES III: Morrowind]`
- **Night Mother controversy:** Some texts claim the Night Mother (patron of the Dark Brotherhood) is Mephala. `[CONTESTED]` — per in-game book **"The Brothers of Darkness"**, this is believed by some but denied by others. No single official source confirms it definitively. ⚠️ **Do not treat this as settled canon in mod content.**

### 10. Meridia `[CANON — UESP]`
> **Source:** UESP Lore:Meridia — https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Meridia

- **Sphere:** Life, infinite energies, living light, the energy of all living things
- **Plane:** The Colored Rooms (a realm of shifting light and colour)
- **Key artifacts:** Dawnbreaker (a sword of holy light that destroys undead on kills)
- **Relation to mortals:** Adversary of the undead; has particular hatred for **necromancy** as a perversion of life-energy. Can be demanding and self-righteous toward her champions.
- **History:** Was once a **Magna-Ge** (a being of pure Aetherius) before being cast out, making her origin unusual among Daedric Princes. `[SOFT CANON — UESP, some debate]`
- **Mod note:** Meridia invasion weight = **1** in this mod's Daedric invasion pool. `[MOD CHOICE]`

### 11. Molag Bal `[CANON — UESP]`
> **Source:** UESP Lore:Molag Bal — https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Molag_Bal

- **Sphere:** Domination, enslavement, vampirism, corruption of the mortal spirit
- **Plane:** **Coldharbour** — a cold, grey-blue mirror of Nirn, drained of all colour, warmth, and hope. It is a twisted reflection of Tamriel: cities exist but are decrepit ruins; seas exist but are stagnant and black; light exists but is pale and joyless. The souls of those who deal with Molag Bal or who die in certain circumstances related to his schemes may be trapped there. His fortress is **Molag Amur** (his palace in this realm). Slave-spirit constructs called **Cold-Flame Atronachs** (blue fire rather than orange) serve him, as do vast armies of Daedra and enslaved souls. `[CANON — ESO main quest; UESP: Lore:Coldharbour]`
- **Key artifacts:** Mace of Molag Bal
- **Title:** "**King of Corruption**" `[CANON — UESP]` ⚠️ Older translations rendered this as "King of Rape" — this is both an outdated English translation and considered inappropriate phrasing in modern usage. **Use "King of Corruption"** in all mod content.
- **Vampirism:** Molag Bal created the first vampire (**Lamae Bal**) in the Merethic Era, making him the ultimate progenitor of all vampirism. `[CANON — ESO, UESP]`
- **Planemeld:** Molag Bal's primary goal across multiple eras is the **Planemeld** — merging Coldharbour with Nirn so he can dominate both. Featured in ESO's main storyline (2E 582). `[CANON — ESO]`

### 12. Namira `[CANON — UESP]`
> **Source:** UESP Lore:Namira — https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Namira

- **Sphere:** Ancient Darkness, decay, revulsion, the rejected and repulsive, the unseen
- **Plane:** The Scuttling Void
- **Key artifacts:** Ring of Namira
- **Title:** "Lady of Decay"
- **Relation to mortals:** Patron of those who live in filth, darkness, and on the margins of society. Associated with cannibalism. Worshipped by some primitive peoples as a goddess of night.

### 13. Nocturnal `[CANON — UESP]`
> **Source:** UESP Lore:Nocturnal — https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nocturnal

- **Sphere:** Night, darkness, mystery, luck, shadow, the unknown
- **Plane:** Evergloam (an eternal twilight realm)
- **Key artifacts:** Skeleton Key (cannot be broken), Gray Cowl of Nocturnal
- **Relation to mortals:** Patron of thieves. Her inner circle of mortal champions are the **Nightingales**, who pledge their soul to guard the **Twilight Sepulchre** in exchange for power in life. `[CANON — TES V: Skyrim]`
- **Title:** "Ur-dra" — claimed by some sources to be the oldest female entity in existence. `[SOFT CANON — UESP]`

### 14. Peryite `[CANON — UESP]`
> **Source:** UESP Lore:Peryite — https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Peryite

- **Sphere:** Pestilence, disease, natural order on a base level, taskmasters
- **Plane:** The Pits (a place of sickness and toil)
- **Key artifacts:** Spell Breaker
- **Title:** "The Taskmaster"
- **Notes:** Despite ruling disease and pestilence, Peryite is considered one of the weakest Daedric Princes in terms of power and prestige among the Princes themselves. `[CANON — UESP]` His appearance (a dragon-like form) is relatively unusual compared to other Princes.

### 15. Sanguine `[CANON — UESP]`
> **Source:** UESP Lore:Sanguine — https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sanguine

- **Sphere:** Hedonism, debauchery, darker desires, revelry, sin
- **Plane:** Many small pocket realms (no single grand realm — befitting his scattered nature)
- **Key artifacts:** Sanguine Rose (summons a Dremora)
- **Title:** "Lord of Revelry"
- **Relation to mortals:** Generally jovial and non-threatening by Daedric standards. Prefers to lead mortals into indulgence rather than cause direct harm.

### 16. Sheogorath `[CANON — UESP]`
> **Source:** UESP Lore:Sheogorath — https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sheogorath

- **Sphere:** Madness, chaos, the irrational, the unpredictable
- **Plane:** The Shivering Isles (split between Mania — manic joy — and Dementia — paranoid despair)
- **Key artifacts:** Wabbajack, Staff of Sheogorath
- **Identity:** ⚠️ Sheogorath IS **Jyggalag** transformed by the other Princes' curse. `[CANON — TES IV: Shivering Isles]` The "Sheogorath" of TES V: Skyrim is the **Champion of Cyrodiil** who took the mantle after defeating Jyggalag. `[CANON — TES IV/V]`
- **Jyggalag (original form):** Prince of Order, Logic, and Prediction — crystalline constructs, perfect systems, absolute predictability. Cursed by the other Princes (who feared him) to become his opposite. Every era, Jyggalag returned during the **Greymarch** to destroy the Shivering Isles, then reverted to Sheogorath. The Champion of Cyrodiil broke this cycle at 3E 433. Jyggalag is now free as a separate entity. `[CANON — TES IV: Shivering Isles; UESP Lore:Jyggalag]`
- **Mantling note:** The Hero of Kvatch mantled Sheogorath by completing the Shivering Isles quest — the most explicit canonical example of **mortal → Daedric Prince** mantling in the series. `[CANON — TES IV: Shivering Isles; confirmed by TES V: Skyrim dialogue]`
- **Relation to mortals:** Wildly unpredictable. Interactions range from amusing to horrifying to both simultaneously. Worshipped and feared in equal measure.

### 17. Vaermina `[CANON — UESP]`
> **Source:** UESP Lore:Vaermina — https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Vaermina

- **Sphere:** Dreams, nightmares, the night, omens, stolen memories
- **Plane:** Quagmire (a nightmare realm where reality constantly shifts to worst fears)
- **Key artifacts:** Skull of Corruption (copies and corrupts enemies), Dreamstride potion
- **Relation to mortals:** Sends nightmares; collects stolen memories. Her realm bleeds into mortal sleep. Worshipped by those who traffic in dreams and visions.
