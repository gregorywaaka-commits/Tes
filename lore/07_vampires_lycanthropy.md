## Vampirism — Origin `[CANON — ESO, UESP]`

> **Source:** UESP Lore:Vampire (Origin section)  
> **Source:** ESO Main Quest (Lamae Bal's story)

- **Molag Bal** (Daedric Prince of Domination/Corruption) created vampirism in the **Merethic Era** by assaulting a **Nedic monk** named **Lamae Beolfag**.
- Lamae died from the assault but rose as the first vampire — the **Blood Matron**, also called **Lamae Bal**. `[CANON — ESO]`
- Lamae's curse spread through biting, creating all subsequent vampire bloodlines. All Tamrielic vampires ultimately descend from this original act. `[CANON — UESP]`
- ⚠️ The specific mechanism by which sub-bloodlines formed (geographic isolation, magical mutation, individual sires) is not fully explained in canon. The general descent from Lamae is `[CANON]`; specific bloodline origins are often `[SOFT CANON]`.

## Vampiric Diseases `[CANON — UESP]`

| Disease | Region | Games |
|---|---|---|
| **Porphyric Hemophilia** | Morrowind | TES III: Morrowind |
| **Sanguinare Vampiris** | Cyrodiil, Skyrim | TES IV: Oblivion, TES V: Skyrim |
| **Noxiphilic Sanguivoria** | Tamriel-wide (ESO) | ESO |

- Vampirism is transmitted through a **bite** or wound infection. Full transformation takes **3 in-game days** if untreated. `[CANON — TES III, IV, V]`
- Vampires are generally **weakened by sunlight** (except in Morrowind, where the debuff is less severe and more nuanced in the in-game texts). `[CANON]`
- **Vampire Lords** are apex pure-blood vampires capable of a partial transformation into a more powerful form. Only true direct bloodline members (e.g., Lord Harkon, Serana) are pure-bloods. `[CANON — TES V: Dawnguard]`

## Vampire Bloodlines

### Lamae Bal (Blood Matron) `[CANON — ESO]`
> **Source:** ESO main quest; UESP Lore:Lamae Bal

- **Region:** Tamriel-wide (origin)
- **Era:** Merethic Era
- **Role:** The first vampire; progenitor of all bloodlines
- **Notes:** Not a "bloodline" in the gameplay sense — she is the ultimate ancestor. Her curse mutated over centuries into the distinct regional bloodlines.

### Volkihar Clan `[CANON — TES V: Dawnguard]`
> **Source:** TES V: Skyrim — Dawnguard DLC; UESP Lore:Volkihar

- **Region:** Skyrim; northern lakes and coastal areas
- **Era of formation:** Early First Era or Merethic Era `[SOFT CANON — no precise date given]`
- **Focus:** Martial dominance; aristocratic hierarchy
- **Traits:** Volkihar lurk beneath frozen lakes. Pure-blood Volkihar (such as **Lord Harkon** and **Serana**) can become **Vampire Lords** — a powerful winged form. `[CANON — TES V: Dawnguard]`
- **Key figures:** Lord Harkon (antagonist of Dawnguard), Serana (daughter; key companion), Valerica (Serana's mother; imprisoned herself in the Soul Cairn). `[CANON — TES V: Dawnguard]`
- **Notes:** Directly trace lineage to Molag Bal's curse via Lamae. `[CANON — UESP]`

### Vampyrum Order `[CANON — ESO / UESP]`
> **Source:** ESO in-game texts; UESP Lore:Vampyrum Order

- **Region:** **Cyrodiil** ⚠️ NOT Morrowind — the Vampyrum Order is a **Cyrodilic** organisation. This is a common point of confusion.
- **Era of formation:** First Era or earlier `[SOFT CANON]`
- **Focus:** Noble infiltration; court politics; servants of Molag Bal
- **Traits:** The Vampyrum Order positions itself as the oldest and most prestigious vampire organisation. Members are deeply embedded in Imperial society as advisors, merchants, and nobles.
- **Relation to Molag Bal:** Active servants; consider themselves his chosen among vampires. `[CANON — ESO]`

### Quarra Clan `[CANON — TES III: Morrowind]`
> **Source:** TES III: Morrowind; in-game book: Vampires of Vvardenfell Vol. I

- **Region:** Northwest Vvardenfell, Morrowind (stronghold: **Druscashti**)
- **Focus:** Warrior; direct physical aggression; frenzy in battle
- **Membership:** Leans toward **Nordic** ancestry
- **Notes:** Most physically aggressive of the three Morrowind clans. View the other clans with contempt. Rivals of Aundae and Berne. `[CANON — TES III: Morrowind]`
- **Status by 3E 427:** In decline due to inter-clan conflict and persecution. `[CANON — TES III: Morrowind]`

### Aundae Clan `[CANON — TES III: Morrowind]`
> **Source:** TES III: Morrowind; in-game book: Vampires of Vvardenfell Vol. I

- **Region:** Western Vvardenfell, Morrowind (stronghold: **Ashmelech**)
- **Focus:** Magic; illusion; hypnosis; arcane power
- **Membership:** Leans toward **Altmer** (High Elf) ancestry
- **Notes:** Elitist and magically formidable. Use hypnosis and mysticism as primary tools. View themselves as superior to the other clans. Rivals of Quarra and Berne. `[CANON — TES III: Morrowind]`

### Berne Clan `[CANON — TES III: Morrowind]`
> **Source:** TES III: Morrowind; in-game book: Vampires of Vvardenfell Vol. II

- **Region:** Southern Vvardenfell, Morrowind (stronghold: **Galom Daeus**)
- **Focus:** Stealth; ambush; poison; assassination
- **Membership:** Leans toward **Imperial** ancestry
- **Notes:** Most "classic vampire" in methodology — prefer to strike unseen and from shadows. Masters of subterfuge. Rivals of Quarra and Aundae. `[CANON — TES III: Morrowind]`

### The Three Morrowind Clans — Relations `[CANON — TES III: Morrowind]`
- The three clans are **mutually hostile**; they cooperate only under extreme necessity. `[CANON]`
- Vampirism is **reviled** in Dunmeri society — living openly as a vampire is impossible in Morrowind. `[CANON — TES III: Morrowind]`
- All three clans are considered to be in decline by 3E 427 due to inter-clan strife, Tribunal Temple persecution, and Imperial pressure. `[CANON — TES III: Morrowind]`

### Gray Host `[CANON — ESO: Greymoor]`
> **Source:** ESO: Greymoor DLC; UESP Lore:Gray Host

- **Region:** Hammerfell and Bangkorai region (later Skyrim in ESO: Greymoor, 2E 582 flashback)
- **Era of formation:** Late First Era; **defeated c. 1E 1029**
- **Focus:** Conquest; mixed vampire and werewolf army
- **Leader:** **King Styriche** of Verkarth; served **Molag Bal**
- **Defeat:** Broken at **Bangkorai Garrison** (1E 1029) by **Empress Hestra** of the Alessian Empire, whose legions drove the Gray Host westward through High Rock to Bangkorai Pass. The battle was turned by the sacrifice of **Saint Pelin**, whose martyrdom is credited with saving the Garrison. `[CANON — ESO in-game book: Bangkorai, Shield of High Rock; UESP: Lore:Gray Host]` ⚠️ Previous entry cited "The Second Akaviri Invasion" — that is about Dir-Kamal at 2E 572, a completely different event 1,500 years later.
- **Later events:** Remnants of the Gray Host resurface in ESO: Greymoor (set 2E 582) when **Rada al-Saran** attempts to resurrect the Host. `[CANON — ESO: Greymoor]`
- **Notes:** Notable for combining **vampires and werewolves** in a single military force — an extremely unusual alliance given the typical rivalry between the two types. `[CANON — ESO]`

## Lycanthropy (Werewolves) `[CANON — UESP]`

> **Primary source:** UESP Lore:Werewolf — https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Werewolf  
> **Source:** TES V: Skyrim (The Companions questline)

### Origin `[CANON — UESP]`
- Lycanthropy was created by **Hircine** (Daedric Prince of the Hunt) as his gift/curse to mortals — his finest hunters and most dangerous prey. `[CANON — TES V: Skyrim]`
- **Transmitted** through bites from other werebeasts. `[CANON]`
- Multiple forms exist beyond werewolves: **werebears** (Solstheim), **werecrocodiles** (Black Marsh), **werelions** (Elsweyr), **wererats**, etc. All are Hircine's creations. `[CANON — UESP]`

### Afterlife `[CANON — TES V: Skyrim]`
- Lycanthropes who die while bearing the curse go to **Hircine's Hunting Grounds** in Oblivion — not to their racial afterlife (Sovngarde for Nords, etc.). `[CANON — TES V: Skyrim, Dawnguard]`
- This is considered by many lycanthropes a blessing (eternal hunt) and by others a curse (denied access to their people's heaven). `[CANON — TES V: Skyrim]`
- The curse can be removed by Hircine's favour or by specific quests; upon removal the person regains their normal afterlife prospects. `[CANON — TES V: Skyrim]`

### The Companions `[CANON — TES V: Skyrim]`
- The **Circle** (inner council of the Companions of Whiterun) maintain a secret tradition of werewolfism. `[CANON — TES V: Skyrim]`
- This tradition was established through a pact with **Hircine** at some point in the Companions' history. `[CANON — TES V: Skyrim]`
- Regular Companions members are unaware of this until inducted into the Circle. `[CANON — TES V: Skyrim]`

### Gray Host `[CANON — ESO: Greymoor]`
- The Gray Host (see Vampires section) uniquely combined vampires **and werewolves** under King Styriche's command. `[CANON — ESO]`
- Both types served Molag Bal, which is what made the alliance possible despite the usual rivalry between the two. `[CANON — ESO]`
