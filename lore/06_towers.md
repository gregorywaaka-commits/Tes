## What Are the Towers? `[CANON — UESP]`

The **Towers** are metaphysical (and usually physical) structures that **stabilise Mundus** and prevent it from dissolving back into the formless void of Oblivion. Each Tower has a **Stone** — a focal artifact that powers it. `[CANON — UESP]`

The concept of Towers is first explicitly discussed in texts like **"The Towers"** and is implied in earlier lore about the construction of Ada-Mantia. The metaphysics are complicated and some details remain `[CONTESTED]` or `[SPECULATION]`.

Key principles `[CANON — UESP]`:
- Altering or destroying a Tower's Stone can have catastrophic effects on Mundus reality in that region.
- Towers were built by various civilisations in imitation of **Ada-Mantia** (the first Tower).
- Some Towers are "active" (Stone intact); others are "fallen" (Stone removed or destroyed).
- The most commonly cited list has **eight Towers**, though some sources hint at more. `[SOFT CANON]`

## The Eight Towers

### 1. Ada-Mantia (Adamantine Tower / Direnni Tower) `[CANON — UESP]`
> **Source:** UESP Lore:Ada-Mantia — https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ada-Mantia

- **Location:** Isle of Balfiera, Iliac Bay, High Rock
- **Stone:** **Zero Stone** — the first and most fundamental Stone
- **Builder:** The **Aedra** themselves, at the **Convention** (end of the Dawn Era)
- **Status:** **Intact** (as of known lore)
- **Significance:** The Ur-Tower; the model for all subsequent Towers. The oldest known constructed structure in Tamriel. The Convention that ended the Dawn Era was held here. `[CANON — UESP]`
- **CK3 mod county:** `c_balfiera` `[MOD CHOICE]`
- **Notes:** The Zero Stone is considered the most powerful and fundamental Stone. Its nature is not well understood. `[SOFT CANON — UESP]`

### 2. White-Gold Tower `[CANON — UESP]`
> **Source:** UESP Lore:White-Gold Tower — https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:White-Gold_Tower

- **Location:** Imperial City isle, Cyrodiil (the tall central spire visible throughout Cyrodiil)
- **Stone:** **Chim-el Adabal** — the **Amulet of Kings**
- **Builder:** The **Ayleids**, in imitation of Ada-Mantia
- **Status:** **Damaged/altered** — the Amulet of Kings was destroyed by Martin Septim (3E 433) to summon Akatosh's avatar. The Tower's metaphysical function is unclear post-Oblivion Crisis. `[CANON — TES IV: Oblivion]`
- **Significance:** Used by the Empire for the **Dragonfires pact** — the ritual of lighting the Dragonfires with the Amulet kept Oblivion gates sealed. `[CANON — TES IV: Oblivion]`
- **CK3 mod county:** `c_imperial_city` `[MOD CHOICE]`

### 3. Red Tower (Red Mountain) `[CANON — UESP]`
> **Source:** UESP Lore:Red Mountain — https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Red_Mountain

- **Location:** Vvardenfell, Morrowind
- **Stone:** **Heart of Lorkhan** — the actual heart of the slain/cast-out et'Ada Lorkhan
- **Builder:** Not constructed; formed naturally when the Heart of Lorkhan fell from the sky
- **Status:** **Destroyed** — the Heart was destroyed by the Nerevarine in TES III: Morrowind (3E 427). Red Mountain subsequently erupted in 4E 5. `[CANON — TES III: Morrowind; UESP]`
- **Significance:** The Tribunal (Vivec, Almalexia, Sotha Sil) drew their divine power directly from the Heart using Kagrenac's Tools. Destroying the Heart ended their divinity. The Dwemer also used the Heart — their disappearance at the Battle of Red Mountain (c. 1E 700) is related to Kagrenac's activation of his tools. `[CANON — TES III: Morrowind]`
- **CK3 mod county:** `c_red_mountain` `[MOD CHOICE]`

### 4. Crystal-Like-Law (Crystal Tower) `[CANON — UESP]`
> **Source:** UESP Lore:Crystal Tower — https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Crystal_Tower

- **Location:** Summerset Isles (Alinor)
- **Stone:** **Crystal Heart** (exact name uncertain; `[SOFT CANON]`)
- **Builder:** The **Altmer**
- **Status:** **Destroyed** — the Crystal Tower was destroyed during ESO-era events `[CANON — ESO]`. Its loss profoundly shook Altmeri civilisation.
- **Significance:** The symbolic and literal centrepiece of Altmer civilisation; considered one of the grandest structures ever built. Its destruction is a major cultural trauma for the Altmer. `[CANON — ESO]`
- **CK3 mod county:** `c_alinor` `[MOD CHOICE]`

### 5. Snow Throat (Throat of the World) `[CANON — UESP]`
> **Source:** UESP Lore:Snow Throat — https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Snow_Throat

- **Location:** Skyrim (the highest mountain in Tamriel; home of the Greybeards and Paarthurnax)
- **Stone:** **Unknown** — various theories suggest the mountain itself, a buried artifact, or the concept of the Voice. `[SPECULATION — UESP]`
- **Builder:** Unknown; may have formed naturally or been shaped
- **Status:** **Intact** (as of known lore)
- **Significance:** Deeply tied to Nord culture, the Thu'um (Voice magic), and dragon lore. High Hrothgar sits partway up the mountain; the peak (the Throat) is where Paarthurnax dwells. `[CANON — TES V: Skyrim]`
- **CK3 mod county:** `c_whiterun` `[MOD CHOICE]`

### 6. Green-Sap `[CANON — UESP]`
> **Source:** UESP Lore:Green-Sap — https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Green-Sap

- **Location:** Valenwood (associated with the walking city of **Falinesti**)
- **Stone:** **Unknown** — possibly the Bosmer heart-tree, the sap of the graht-oaks, or a buried stone. `[SPECULATION — UESP]`
- **Builder:** Unknown; possibly formed or grown by the Bosmer with Y'ffre's involvement
- **Status:** **Uncertain** — Falinesti reportedly stopped walking at some point in the 3E or 4E. `[SOFT CANON — UESP]`
- **Significance:** Associated with Bosmeri nature magic, the Green Pact, and the life-force of Valenwood's great walking trees. `[CANON — UESP]`
- **CK3 mod county:** `c_falinesti` `[MOD CHOICE]`

### 7. Orichalc Tower `[CANON — UESP]`
> **Source:** UESP Lore:Orichalc Tower — https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Orichalc_Tower

- **Location:** **Yokuda** (sunken continent; Redguard homeland)
- **Stone:** An **orichalcum** artifact (exact form unknown)
- **Builder:** Unknown (Yokudan civilisation)
- **Status:** **Destroyed** — sank with Yokuda c. 1E 792. `[CANON — UESP]`
- **Significance:** Its destruction may have been related to — or even caused — the sinking of Yokuda. `[SPECULATION — UESP]`
- **Mod note:** The Orichalc Tower is intentionally **omitted** from this mod's Tower system because it was destroyed 1E 792, centuries before the mod's playable timeframe. `[MOD CHOICE — documented]`

### 8. Walk-Brass (Numidium) `[CANON — UESP]`
> **Source:** UESP Lore:Numidium — https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Numidium

- **Location:** Mobile; created by the **Dwemer**; first activated by **Tiber Septim** c. 2E 896
- **Stone:** The **Mantella** — a powerful soul gem / heart-stone
- **Builder:** **Dwemer**; designed by **Kagrenac**; intended to use the Heart of Lorkhan as its power source
- **Status:** **Destroyed** — most recently after the events of TES II: Daggerfall (3E 417 **Dragon Break** — the Warp in the West). `[CANON — TES II: Daggerfall]`
- **Significance:** Walk-Brass is unusual — it is a **mobile Tower** in the form of a giant brass golem. It can rewrite reality through brute force. Tiber Septim used it to conquer Tamriel; the Numidium's activation caused a **Dragon Break** (multiple contradictory timelines simultaneously true). `[CANON — UESP]`
- **Dragon Break:** The "**Warp in the West**" at the end of Daggerfall is the most famous Dragon Break — all six endings of TES II: Daggerfall are simultaneously canon. `[CANON — UESP developer confirmation]`
